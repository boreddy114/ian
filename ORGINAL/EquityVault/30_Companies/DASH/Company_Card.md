## One-Liner
DoorDash is transitioning from a 67% U.S. market share delivery marketplace into the operating system for local commerce — our APK teardown found 787+ files of Stripe Terminal POS infrastructure in the production app (growing to 1,300+ in the current build), evidence that the merchant services pillar is further along than the market appreciates. At $169, the 40%+ drawdown prices the 2026 investment cycle without crediting POS optionality, the advertising ramp, or the data intelligence loop.

## Bull Case
1. **POS infrastructure in production code.** 787 files of Stripe Terminal SDK in May 2025 build → 1,300+ in March 2026. Includes COTS phone-as-terminal (73→86 files), BBPos hardware SDK (431→458), offline mode (172→180), Mastercard EMV certification (67 files). Two deployment paths built simultaneously. No sell-side analyst has identified this.
2. **67% U.S. market share = unmatched demand leverage.** A restaurant cannot turn off DoorDash without losing 2/3 of delivery orders. Makes the POS commission-for-payments bundle compelling in a way no standalone POS company can replicate.
3. **Physical tether business mispriced in SaaS downdraft.** Dasher logistics network, 100K+ merchant integrations, SmartScale hardware, DashMart warehouses = atoms that can't be vibe-coded. Being sold alongside Salesforce/Adobe but asset base looks more like FedEx/Visa.
4. **Advertising at $1B+ with structural runway.** Advertisers want to spend more than available inventory allows. POS + CRM closes the loop on consumer behavior → dramatically better ad targeting.
5. **Tony Xu alignment:** 57–69% voting control, $300K salary, $2.3B personal stake, PSU vesting at $188–$501 (current price $169 below first hurdle).

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Extractive economics objection:** 30% delivery commission is one of the highest take rates in commerce. Restaurants view it as a tax. Trust deficit may be too wide to bridge for POS adoption — fox guarding the henhouse.
2. **SBC dilution:** $2.6B SBC vs. $2.78B adj. EBITDA in FY25 = shareholders funding nearly the entire EBITDA through dilution. Even at guided $1.3–1.4B FY26, SBC = 40–50% of adj. EBITDA.
3. **Investment cycle may extend beyond 2026.** Management has not committed to 2027 as payoff year. UBS: "2027 Investment Intensity Remains Unanswered."
4. **POS adoption may be slower than code suggests.** Code ≠ adoption. Replacing a POS is operationally brutal — overnight migrations, staff retraining, workflow overhauls. Path is new openings and switchers, not displacing happy Toast incumbents.
5. **Grocery push as potential capital destruction.** DashMart Fulfillment Services resembles Amazon's failed Fresh playbook — capital-intensive, structurally low-margin. Amazon closed 72 locations in Jan 2026.
