# TKO Thesis Log

## 2026-04-03

### Thesis Established — Buy

**Live sports as the ultimate scarce asset in an AI-abundant world.** TKO owns UFC and WWE — two year-round franchises with no off-season, no stadium ownership, $15B in locked media rights, and multiple visible growth levers. The business compounds at 9-10% on the core sports assets with 50%+ segment margins. At 19x EV/EBITDA, the valuation is fair for a premium sports platform with contractual revenue escalators and a 2033 media rights renewal that could step-function the earnings power.

### Key Analytical Work

- **Bottom-up event tracker:** Built per-event revenue model using face-value ticket pricing, venue capacities, and state athletic commission attendance data. Backtested Q4 2025: estimated $70.3M vs. $72.0M reported live events revenue (2.4% variance). Sell-side models this top-down; our approach gives intra-quarter visibility on the live events line.
- **UP-C resolution:** Traced the full distribution waterfall through sell-side hidden tabs and verified against the 10-K cash flow statement. The UP-C is economically equivalent to a normal C-corp on a per-share basis — zero variance. Reported adjusted FCF imputes a 12% effective tax rate (misleading presentation), but applying a normalized 25-29% rate yields the same per-share economics as a traditional corporation. The governance concerns are about control and dilution, not tax leakage.
- **IMG dilution quantified:** Class A economic interest went from 49% at formation (Sep 2023) to 40.1% at year-end 2025. The 10-K confirms the IMG acquisition caused "an approximately 6% reduction in the relative percentage interest." Common control transaction, no fairness opinion, no shareholder vote.
- **Expert transcript review:** Former Fox Sports and Paramount executives estimate 1.5-2.5x at the 2033 renewal. PFL competitive threat assessed as overstated ($100M Saudi investment insufficient to systematically challenge UFC's roster). FIP structure confirmed as fixed guarantees plus host-subsidized travel/ops costs.
- **CBS simulcast baseline:** UFC 326 averaged 2.47M viewers (Mar 7, 2026) — best UFC linear audience in a decade, +208% in 18-34 demo. Tracking UFC 327 Miami (Apr 11) and White House event (Jun 14) for trajectory.
- **BEA dataset:** 65 years of live entertainment spending data (FRED series DLIGRC1A027NBEA). Four annual declines since 1959. 2025 reading at $60.4B is an all-time high. Category still just 0.27% of total PCE.

### Thesis Tracker

1. **CBS simulcast trajectory:** 2.5M debut → need 3.5M (Miami) → 6M+ (White House) to validate mainstream broadcast thesis and support 1.5-2x at 2033 Paramount+ renewal.
