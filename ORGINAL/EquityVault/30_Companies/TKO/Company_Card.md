## One-Liner
TKO owns the two dominant year-round live sports franchises (UFC + WWE) — scarce physical assets that compound at 9–10% on visible, largely contractual growth drivers in an era where AI-driven digital abundance makes live experiences structurally more valuable. At 19x EV/EBITDA, the valuation is fair for premium assets with $15B in locked media rights, a 2033 renewal catalyst, and expanding international monetization.

## Bull Case
1. **Live sports = ultimate scarce asset in AI-abundant world.** 65 years of BEA data: only 4 annual declines since 1959. 2025 at $60.4B all-time high, still just 0.27% of total PCE. Structural tailwind from digital abundance → physical scarcity.
2. **Five visible growth levers compound 9–10% annually.** Media rights escalators (contractual 5%/yr), ticket pricing headroom vs. NFL/NBA/F1, international FIPs scaling ($300M+ in 2026, $380–420M by 2030), sponsorship expanding into new categories (25%+ growth), CBS simulcast validating mainstream broadcast thesis.
3. **Bottom-up event tracker provides differentiated analytical edge.** Backtested Q4 2025: $70.3M estimated vs. $72.0M reported (2.4% variance). Per-event data reveals bimodal economics sell-side models miss — numbered Miami event = $10.3M gate vs. Apex card = $0.
4. **UP-C structure is presentation issue, not economic one.** Traced full distribution waterfall: zero variance between Class A FCF and hypothetical C-corp. Real governance concern is control/dilution (49% → 40.1% from IMG deal), not tax mechanics.
5. **CBS simulcast trajectory on track.** UFC 326 debut: 2.47M viewers, best UFC linear audience in a decade, +208% in 18–34 demo. Need 3.5M (Miami) → 6M+ (White House Jun 14) to validate 1.5–2x at 2033 renewal.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Fighter wage inflation.** UFC fighters earn ~20% of event revenue vs. 45–55% in major leagues, 70% in boxing. Sustained move above 25% compresses margins 300–600bp. Antitrust settlement ($335M) may embolden future claims.
2. **UP-C governance risk.** Silver Lake controls 61% of voting. IMG acquisition diluted Class A from 49% → 40.1% via common control transaction — no fairness opinion, no shareholder vote. Could happen again.
3. **CBS viewership plateau risk.** If simulcast flatlines at 2–3M viewers, 2033 renewal is 1.2x not 1.5–2x → reduces 2036 media estimate by ~$500M and base case EV by $5–7B.
4. **Real FCF ~30% lower than reported.** UP-C imputes ~12% effective tax rate; normalized 25–29% rate yields ~$950M real run-rate FCF. Real P/FCF ~22x, not the 16x headline.
5. **PFL competitive threat.** $100M Saudi investment in PFL/Bellator merger creates first credible MMA competitor. LIV Golf-style fighter poaching is a tail risk.
