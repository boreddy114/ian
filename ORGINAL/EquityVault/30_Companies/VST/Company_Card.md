## One-Liner
Vistra is the largest integrated retail electricity and power generation company in the U.S. (~44 GW, 5M retail customers), positioned as the "Goldilocks" IPP with balanced ERCOT/PJM exposure, a 6.4 GW nuclear fleet with hyperscaler PPAs (Amazon, Meta), and asymmetric upside to the AI-driven power demand super-cycle — trading at ~8x FY28E EV/EBITDA, roughly 30% below September 2025 highs despite positive fundamental developments.

## Bull Case
1. **AI power demand super-cycle** — Data centers from ~4% to ~12% of U.S. power demand by 2030; 30-50% peak load increase expected by end of decade.
2. **Nuclear PPAs at premium pricing** — Amazon Comanche Peak (1.2 GW, $95/MWh, 20-year); Meta Perry/Davis-Besse (2.2 GW, ~$89/MWh + uprates at ~$120/MWh); ~$800M EBITDA at full ramp.
3. **Scarcity premiums on existing assets** — High barriers to new build (permitting, interconnection, equipment shortages); existing fleet benefits from supply constraints.
4. **Balanced ERCOT/PJM exposure** — "Goldilocks" positioning; PJM forwards UP $12-14/MWh (2027-2030); diversified risk.
5. **Shareholder returns** — $6.7B returned since 2021; 30% share count reduction; targeting 30-31% of current market cap returned.
6. **Accretive M&A** — Lotus ($1.9B, ~$743/kW vs. ~$2,700/kW newbuild); Cogentrix (~$4B, 5.5 GW, ~7.25x EV/EBITDA).
7. **FCF yield compression opportunity** — ~11% FY28 FCF yield (ex-buybacks), ~13% with buybacks; significant margin of safety.
8. **BNPP upside case: +35% vs. Street 2030 EBITDA** — $13.9B vs. consensus $10.3B.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **ERCOT forward price weakness** — Forwards down $4-6/MWh near-term from solar/storage additions, batching delays, DC delays.
2. **PJM capacity market reform risk** — Potential migration toward prompt-auction; clearing prices could normalize to ~$200/MW-day from current $329.
3. **Coal exposure** — 20% of capacity is coal; secular headwind, potential regulatory/policy risk.
4. **Hedging strategy caps upside** — "Intentionally aggressive" hedging; over half of 2028 already locked in.
5. **Operational event risk** — Moss Landing battery fire, Martin Lake outages, Winter Storm Uri ($1.6B negative EBITDA in 2021).
6. **~30% drawdown from Sept 2025 highs** — Stock not pricing in upside from future DC contracts (~$20/share in Jefferies PT).
7. **Local DC opposition/moratoriums** — Growing NIMBY resistance to data center build-out.

## Key Monitoring
1. Quarterly PPA announcements (nuclear and gas fleet contracting cadence).
2. PJM capacity auction results and reform proposals (backstop procurement: 14.9 GW UCAP target).
3. ERCOT forward power prices and solar/storage interconnection pace.
4. Cogentrix regulatory approvals (DOJ, FERC; expected close August 2025).
5. SB6 implementation and ERCOT batch interconnection reforms.
6. Hedging profile evolution (currently 96% hedged 2026, 70-84% 2027).
