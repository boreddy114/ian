## Summary

Buy AMZN on the strategic re-positioning that is now visible but not yet modeled by the sell-side: AWS is no longer just "the third hyperscaler" — it is the home for both top frontier labs (OpenAI and Anthropic), and the launch of Bedrock Managed Agents creates an entirely new category of enterprise AI product that AWS has built jointly with OpenAI and exclusively offers through its platform. This is the AI-product analog to AWS's 2006 cloud-launch moment — a new kind of computing infrastructure that customers haven't yet learned to demand at scale. We model $14.00 in FY27 EPS at a 25x multiple to a $350 PT, ~30% upside.

Where we differ from the sell-side: Raymond James models $11.59 GAAP FY27 EPS and a $280 PT (their bull case reaches $350 only on FY28 EPS). Williams Blair models $9.54. Our $14 estimate sits ~21% above Raymond James base, ~47% above Williams Blair, and our $350 PT pulls Raymond James' bull case forward by one year. The delta is concentrated in two places: faster Bedrock Managed Agents revenue contribution as the OpenAI partnership scales, and AWS growth holding above 33% in FY27 as the Anthropic + OpenAI capacity ramps fully monetize. Both are defensible if the strategic re-positioning is real — and the post-print evidence suggests it is.

## Our Thesis

AWS is the open-platform cloud distribution winner for agentic AI infrastructure. Bedrock Managed Agents — co-built with OpenAI and exclusive to AWS — creates a product category with structurally different unit economics from Bedrock's API-call business that the sell-side has not yet modeled as a distinct revenue contributor. Combined with sustained AWS reacceleration from the Anthropic and OpenAI capacity ramps, all-time-high op margin, and a meaningful optionality stack, our $14.00 FY27 EPS estimate sits 21–47% above consensus and supports a $350 price target at a conservative 25x multiple.

## Proof Points

### BMA Is a New Product Category, Not a Feature

BMA is an enterprise-grade runtime for stateful AI agents — with identity, permissions, memory, security boundaries, and AWS VPC integration built in — co-developed by AWS and OpenAI, and exclusive to AWS. Altman: "we're doing this exclusively with Amazon, we're excited about it." Garman: "nobody else has it." Altman frames the broader opportunity as "a completely new kind of computing." Neither Raymond James nor Williams Blair model BMA as a distinct revenue contributor; both fold it into Bedrock generally. The unit economics are structurally different from Bedrock's API-call business and deserve separate modeling.

### Bedrock Best-in-Class for Agentic Orchestration — Confirmed by Practitioners

Multiple independent practitioners describe Microsoft as "less mature on complex orchestration" and Google as "less mature" on enterprise governance. AWS sits in the middle with the most enterprise-ready agentic platform — and now layers on OpenAI's frontier models exclusively through BMA. Clients are "explicitly instructing IT partners to build on Bedrock." The competitive position is the cleanest of the three hyperscalers for agentic workload capture.

### The MSFT-OpenAI Exclusivity Break Created the Structural Opening

MSFT had to give up OpenAI exclusivity because exclusivity was capping OpenAI's growth — enterprises wanted OpenAI inside their AWS data estate, not on Azure. AWS is the structural beneficiary of that asymmetry. The OpenAI relationship is shifting toward Amazon, not just opening up to Amazon — BMA is the proof point of that shift in the form of a flagship co-built product.

### AWS Reaccelerated to 28% on a $150B Base — Fastest Growth in 15 Quarters

Q1 AWS revenue $37.6B, +28% Y/Y — a 480bps Q/Q acceleration. AWS margin held at 37.7%, well above buyside expectations. AWS backlog reached $364B excluding the Anthropic $100B+ deal — effectively ~$464B+ inclusive. Williams Blair flagged backlog +90% Y/Y excluding Anthropic and Meta. Capacity-constrained, not demand-constrained. Jassy: "demand has accelerated since 2H25." Our FY27 AWS growth assumption: 33–34%, vs. Raymond James at 30%. The Anthropic and OpenAI capacity ramps hit through 2H26 and run through FY27 — the math supports continued acceleration into 1H27 before naturally moderating.

### Op Margin Durability Supports the EPS Bridge

Q1 consolidated op margin 13.1%, an all-time high. We model FY27 op margin closer to 15% — above Raymond James base (14.3%), below their bull case (16%). Drivers: AWS margin holding above 35%, retail cost-to-serve still leveraging (Q1 units +15% vs. fulfillment +9%), ads at 22%+ growth on high incremental margin, internal AI productivity contributing material opex leverage (Jassy: 5 AI-forward people rebuilt a service in 65 days vs. 50 people in a year). AWS contributes ~59% of consolidated EBIT — each 100bps of AWS margin or growth surprise translates to ~3.5x the consolidated EBIT impact of the same retail surprise.

### Optionality Stack Adds to the FY27/28 Case

**Amazon Leo:** commercial launch Q3:26. Delta, JetBlue, AT&T, Vodafone, NASA, and Apple iPhone/Watch satellite services already signed. Q4 begins capitalizing satellite costs — removes a P&L drag. Jassy: "many billion-dollar revenue business" longer-term.

**Prime price hike:** mid-2026 likely — fourth year at current pricing. ~$5B annualized OI benefit. Williams Blair flags as a potential 2026 catalyst.

**Whole Foods + grocery:** >$150B grocery GMV in 2025, perishables +40x Y/Y, 100 new Whole Foods stores coming. Same-day perishable customers spend 80%+ more.

**Ads at $17.2B / +22%:** growing meaningfully faster than retail with high incremental margin. Rufus/agentic commerce monetization could push this higher in FY27.

**$350 PT = 25x our FY27 EPS of $14.** That multiple sits below where MSFT and GOOG trade and is conservative if AWS sustains 30%+ growth into FY28 (Raymond James base case at $9.54 sells at 28x; their bull case implies 23x on FY28 at $13.55–15.52). The multiple compression we are underwriting is intentional — we want the upside to come from earnings, not from multiple expansion that requires a re-rating story to play out perfectly.

### Capex as Feature, Not Bug

TTM FCF collapsed to $1.2B (–95% Y/Y). Jassy framed this as the AWS-launch-era playbook: capex 6–24 months ahead of monetization, customer commitments already underwriting a substantial portion of capacity. Raymond James models FCF returning to $6.3B in FY27. The capex IS the bull case — it is funding the OpenAI and Anthropic ramps that drive AWS reacceleration into FY27. This is a capital cycle trough, not structural deterioration.

### Trainium: Recalibrated

Our AlphaSense expert work confirms Trainium2 benchmarks against Nvidia H100, not H200/B200 — "not even in the same picture." Prior AWS attempt to serve Claude 3.5 Sonnet on Trainium for code-gen was "unreliable and ultimately failed." We have removed Trainium as a load-bearing thesis pillar. The buy case does not require Trainium to be an Nvidia killer; it requires AWS to be the open-platform cloud distribution winner with the agentic product category moat (BMA), which it is. Trainium serves as cost-optimized capacity for managed-service workloads — a margin contributor, not a competitive differentiation.

### Capital Return Anomaly

AMZN does not return capital — a structural anomaly vs. mega-cap peers. The absence is real but also a free option. Any resumption of buyback or dividend initiation would be a multi-day catalyst.

## What Could Go Wrong

1. **AWS growth decelerates in Q2 or Q3.** If AWS falls below 26% before the Anthropic/OpenAI capacity ramps hit, the reacceleration thesis loses credibility. This is the single most important quarterly data point to watch. A deceleration would invalidate the entire earnings bridge.

2. **BMA adoption is slower than expected.** If Q3:26 and Q4:26 produce no material agentic revenue contribution and no new customer logos, the sell-side consensus may be right that BMA is a feature, not a product category. The differentiated EPS estimate depends on this being a distinct and monetizable layer.

3. **OpenAI or Anthropic diversify compute away from AWS at scale.** Both relationships carry concentration risk. Any public signal of meaningful workload migration to Azure or GCP would compress the AWS backlog outlook and reprice the exclusivity premium embedded in our estimate.

4. **FCF remains at or below $1B TTM through Q3:26.** If capex normalization does not begin by Q4:26, the AWS-launch-playbook narrative breaks down. This would not kill the thesis but would extend the timeline and invite multiple compression on FCF-based valuation frameworks.
