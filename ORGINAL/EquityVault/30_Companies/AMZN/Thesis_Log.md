## 2026-05-01 — IC Memo: Buy Recommendation (Open Platform Thesis)

**Source:** IC Memo "Two Models, Two Buys: AMZN as Open Platform, GOOG as Vertical Integration" (May 1, 2026)
**Rating:** Buy — conviction high but below GOOG
**Price at write:** ~$255

### Core Thesis

AMZN is the **open-platform play** on secular AI infrastructure spend. AWS contributes 59% of consolidated EBIT on 21% of revenue, reaccelerated to 28% Y/Y on a $150B base (fastest growth in 15 quarters), and the Bedrock orchestration layer is validated by experts as best-in-class for multi-model agentic workflows. The buy case is AWS as cloud distribution, not AWS as merchant silicon competitor.

### Why Conviction Is Below GOOG

Expert pressure-test work surfaced three differentiators:

1. **TPU is competitive; Trainium isn't (yet).** ~1M TPUs in production for Anthropic vs. <1% of Anthropic inference on Trainium. Trainium software ecosystem described as "where AMD ROCm was 3 years ago."
2. **GOOG's Cloud margin expansion is structurally validated today; AMZN's margin story is partly future-tense.** GOOG's 33% Cloud margin is delivered and explained. AMZN's 13.1% all-time-high is real but Trainium inference-margin upside has more execution risk than the call implied.
3. **Vertical-integration moat is harder to replicate than open-platform moat.** GOOG owns silicon + model + platform + distribution. AMZN's moat (governance, distribution, partner ecosystem) is valuable but each component faces direct competition.

### What Expert Work Confirmed (Bull)

- Bedrock is best-in-class for agent-to-agent orchestration — programmability, operational resilience, security guardrails, multimodal management, consistent APIs
- AWS wins regulated industries and cybersecurity workloads — clients "explicitly instructing IT partners to build on Bedrock"
- OpenAI on AWS is real — Altman: "We're doing this exclusively with Amazon, we're excited about it." ~2GW OpenAI Trainium capacity ramping in 2027
- Op margin all-time high on demonstrated operating leverage. Each 100bps of AWS growth surprise → ~3.5x EBIT impact vs. equivalent retail surprise
- Optionality stack broader than peers (Leo, Whole Foods expansion, Prime price hike, Trainium racks)

### What Expert Work Flagged (Bear)

- Trainium2 benchmarks against Nvidia H100, not H200/B200 — "not even in the same picture"
- Prior AWS attempt to serve Claude 3.5 Sonnet on Trainium for code-gen was "unreliable and ultimately failed"
- "Top 3 chip business globally" ($20B run rate) includes Graviton + Nitro — Trainium-specific revenue materially smaller
- Racks-as-product "physically and architecturally locked into AWS Nitro system and Neuron compiler toolchain"
- FCF collapsed to $1.2B TTM (–95% Y/Y) — more severe than call language emphasized

### Position Sizing

Modestly overweight. Buy on weakness, particularly pullbacks from FCF anxiety, Q2 fuel-cost noise, or tariff headlines. Entry at $255 reasonable; would size up more aggressively below $245. Time horizon: 12–18 months.

## 2026-04-30 — Buy Pitch Drafted (Post Q1)

**Source:** Internal one-pager, post-Q1 print
**Rating:** Buy | Arete PT $301 (~18% upside from $255)

Thesis in one line: AMZN is being mispriced as a capex-heavy cloud catch-up name when it has just become a vertically integrated AI infrastructure business with a top-3 global silicon franchise, an accelerating cloud business on a $150B run rate, and an all-time-high consolidated op margin — all while trading at a discount to peers without an AI premium baked in.

Four pillars validated by Q1: (1) AWS reacceleration to 28%, (2) Trainium franchise >$20B run rate with $225B+ commitments, (3) op margin all-time high at 13.1%, (4) optionality stack (Leo, racks-as-product, Whole Foods, Prime price hike).

Bear rebuttal: FCF collapse is transient (AWS-launch playbook, 30+ year asset lives). AWS "miss" vs. buyside whisper of 30%+ is misleading — 28% on $150B base with 480bps acceleration is a better setup. Fuel/tariff costs manageable against AWS/ads margin acceleration.
