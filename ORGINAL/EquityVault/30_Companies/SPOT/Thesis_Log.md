## April 28, 2026 — Q1'26 Earnings: Solid Fundamentals, 2Q Guide Disappoints

**Source:** Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript (Bloomberg); Raymond James SPOT Comment (Apr 28, 2026)

### Headline
Revenue €4,533M (+14% FXN), Gross Margin 33.0% (beat by 20bps), Op Income €715M (beat by €55M, partly social charges). But 2Q guide underwhelmed: GM 33.1% (vs. 33.3% consensus), Op Income €630M (vs. €678M). Management taking "calculated investments" in AI, new products, and marketing without detail until May 21 Investor Day. Stock ~$427, down -45% from 52-week high of $785.

### AI & Product Highlights
- **DJ:** 94M subscribers, closing in on 100M, billions of hours of engagement
- **SongDNA:** 52M users in just 4 weeks post-launch
- **Taste Profile (beta):** Users can directly edit how Spotify models their listening — "passive to interactive"
- **Prompted Playlists:** Now includes podcasts, not just music
- **Jam:** Usage doubled YoY, >100M monthly listening hours
- **Large Personalization Model:** Proprietary taste model trained on 700M+ users — "taste is not a fact, it's an opinion"
- **Fitness Hub:** Launched with Peloton premium content. 70% of Premium users work out monthly; 150M+ workout playlists exist

### Pricing Power
- US price increase (Jan '26): "No surprises at all" — no unusual churn
- Good-better-best tiering in select markets showing "structural increase in ARPU" (very early)
- ARPU expansion tools: audiobook add-ons, tiering, pricing, engagement-driven LTV growth

### Ad Business — Still a Work in Progress
- Biddable now >1/3 of ad revenue, growing quickly
- Legacy direct sales channel still choppy
- Management expects improved growth in H2'26 as biddable scales
- Ad GM dipped slightly in Q1 — engagement growing faster than ad monetization (short-term mismatch)

### Cost Discipline
- Headcount -65 Q/Q; flat for 3 years
- Compute per employee up, not headcount — "doing much more with roughly the same people"
- OpEx guidance: elevated for next 1-2 quarters on AI and marketing; expects full-year improvement

### RJ Estimate Changes (post Q1)
- FY26E Revenue: €19.4B (from €19.5B)
- FY26E Op Income: €2.75B (from €2.90B) — -5%
- FY27E Revenue: €21.8B (from €22.0B)

## January 27, 2026 — MoffettNathanson Initiation: Neutral, $487 PT

**Source:** Exile on Wall Street Recorded Music Initiation (MoffettNathanson, Jan 27, 2026)

### Core Thesis
MoffettNathanson initiates SPOT at **Neutral ($487 PT)** — respects the dominance but sees near-term gross margin risk from fresh label deals. Prefers labels (WMG Buy $38, UMG Buy €26) as better risk/reward for the "it's the labels' turn" thesis.

### Key Arguments Against SPOT

**1. Gross Margin Expansion at Risk:**
- All three Majors (UMG, WMG, Sony) renewed deals in 2024-2025 with wholesale rate escalators
- WMG explicitly guided to calendar 2026 wholesale rate benefits
- Street models +130bps GM expansion for each of next 3 years — MN skeptical of monotonic improvement
- The audiobook/MLC bundling gambit (halved US publishing royalties in 2024) has been reversed by direct deals
- Q3'25 saw marginal royalty cost growth snap back above 60% after Sony renewal

**2. Ad Business Structurally Constrained:**
- Digital audio is ~$8B in US ($1B in Europe) vs. $260B+ total digital ads
- SPOT gets ~10 min screen time/day vs. YouTube/TikTok at ~80 min — 19x aggregate engagement gap
- Video pivot risks alienating music-first users — "trying to be YouTube risks ceding audio dominance"
- Podcast ad growth decelerating; political year benefit nonrecurring
- SPOT ad growth tracks digital audio market, not digital ad market — "not an execution issue, it's a market issue"

**3. Label Leverage Is Not Dead:**
- 80/0.2 power law: 80% of streams from top 0.2% of tracks. Of 250M tracks in ecosystem, 1M matter
- Music catalogs are homogeneous across SAPs — no exclusivity moat for distributors
- Apple and YouTube can subsidize music within bundles (neither needs standalone economics)
- Labels secured AI exclusion clauses in recent deals

### Key Arguments For SPOT (acknowledged by MN)
- Undisputed SAP champion at ~40% market share
- Platform-agnostic distribution advantage
- AI recommendation engine could be genuine differentiation (but parallels to Pandora's Music Genome cautioned)
- Significant free cash flow generation now established

### Industry Framework (relevant to SPOT thesis)
- US households spend just 3% of entertainment budget on music (vs. 8% in peak CD era) — room for pricing
- 67% of US households have paid music sub — penetration gains slowing, pricing must take over
- 60% of subscriber base from $100K+ households — low price elasticity for developed market core
- MN models ~12% revenue CAGR for SPOT over next 3 years

### MN Valuation: $487 PT
- ~29x '26E EBITDA, ~24x '27E EBITDA
- 43x '26E P/E, 34x '27E P/E
- Derived primarily from unlevered DCF + EV/EBITDA
