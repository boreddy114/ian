### EPS Bridge: What Flattered the Beat
- Tax benefit from share-based awards: **$895M reduction to tax provision = $2.91 EPS uplift, +310bps ROE**
- Effective tax rate: 13.2% vs. 21.4% full-year 2025
- Record $5.0B buyback (up from $3.0B in Q4-25); avg diluted shares -1.6%
- Normalize for tax and the beat narrows substantially

### Earnings Call Key Themes (David Solomon)

**Macro environment shifting**
- Q1 began with optimism (record highs, clients focused on growth and strategic activity)
- As quarter progressed, macro environment weighed on sentiment; volatility increased meaningfully with tariff concerns
- "Things rarely move in a straight line"

**IB pipeline remains constructive**
- Backlog decreased slightly but ecosystem remains "pretty constructive"
- Sponsor/PE activity still depressed — sponsor monetization slower due to economic incentives and optionality
- Solomon: rough enterprise value of all PE-owned companies is "like $4 trillion — less than one Nvidia"
- Expects more PE activity "over time" but timing uncertain

**Equities financing structural growth**
- Client demand for equity financing remains "tremendous"
- GS stepping up capital deployment to support client activity, driving higher VaR
- "Slight shift in the mixed profile" toward very attractive sub-segments

**AWM scaling**
- 23% pre-tax margin vs. 30% target still a work-in-progress
- Long-term fee-based inflows continue (33 consecutive quarters)

### Barclays Assessment (Jason Goldberg)

- **Rating: Overweight | PT: $1,048** (+15.4% upside from $908)
- EPS beat aided by lower tax rate (-290bps y/y) and comp ratio (-100bps y/y)
- Equities financing and IB fees (M&A, ECM, DCM) better than expected
- FICC and AWM were worse than expected
- Record buyback of $5B; average diluted shares -1.6%
- Capital ratios fell — CET1 down 180bps is a notable watch item

## April 13, 2026 — Earnings Call: Sponsor Activity & FICC Color

**Source:** 479018D7-...pdf (Bloomberg transcript)

### Notable Q&A Points

**On sponsor/PE activity (Mike Mayo question)**
- Sponsor activity as % of IB not disclosed but is "meaningfully smaller percentage" in a strong M&A quarter
- PE-owned companies: ~$4T enterprise value "less than one Nvidia" for context
- Economic incentives favor waiting; LP pressure mounting but GPs taking optionality
- Solomon expects more activity over time; very well-positioned when it turns on

**On FICC softness (Saul Martinez question)**
- Rates and mortgages weakness is market backdrop, not Goldman-specific
- VaR went up in rates and commodities — mathematically driven by higher rolling 30-day volatility
- Not a structural concern per management

**On equities financing growth (Denis Coleman)**
- Client demand for equity financing remains strong despite market volatility
- GS deploying more capital, shift toward attractive sub-segments
- Activity levels could reverse if markets draw down significantly, but persistent demand so far

**On capital deployment**
- Record $5B buyback signals management confidence
- Balance between supporting client activity (RWA growth) and returning capital to shareholders
- CET1 buffer narrowing to 110bps is a constraint to monitor
