### New Evidence Integrated
- **Howard Mason / RenMac (3 notes, Feb–Apr 2026):** "Battle for upstream control" — identity resolution moving upstream from network to agent layer; clearance commoditization thesis; mandate-risk underwriting as new rent pool. Mason argues PayPal Agent Ready is the only bilateral underwriter model that scales. Intellectually rigorous but suspiciously monotonic toward PayPal.
- **MA Director of Product Development (AlphaSense, Apr 1, 2026):** Confirms stablecoin remains B2B/institutional at consumer level. Dismissive of agentic disintermediation but does not engage Mason's architectural unbundling argument. Names FedNow, PIX, Saudi Mada, inter-European equivalents as real threats in cash-to-cashless markets. "They are going from cash to their local payment rate directly without Visa, Mastercard, Amex, or Discover in between. This is new."
- **Former Visa VP of Payment Product Modernization (AlphaSense, Dec 8, 2025):** 32 years at Visa, ~118 patents. Confirms protocol bifurcation: LLM platforms own purchase protocol; networks own payment protocol. "There's an opportunity in agentic for the issuers and the payment systems to truly take back what they lost when e-commerce came to be." Networks cannot displace processors — "merchants and servicing merchants is a highly customized kind of environment."
- **Stripe 2025 Annual Letter (Feb 24, 2026):** Tempo blockchain launched (with Visa testing); industry at agentic "levels 1–2" of 5-level taxonomy; stablecoin payments volume doubled to ~$400B (60% B2B); Bridge volume 4x+ YoY. MA conspicuously absent (zero mentions) while Visa appears multiple times.

### Key Findings
1. **Network moat downgraded to Tier 4–5 borderline** — Tempo + Bridge-Visa cards make rail layer more contestable; clearance commoditization thesis gaining concrete infrastructure
2. **New blind spot: mandate-risk underwriting** — Mason's framework identifies a genuine new loss category orthogonal to existing fraud/credit risk. MA's Verifiable Intent provides evidentiary record but not liability framework. PayPal positioned first.
3. **Cash-to-cashless bypass risk explicit** — MA insider confirms new markets skipping cards entirely; affects international growth narrative
4. **System of Record recalibrated** — Visa VP clarifies stickiness is brand + scheme rules, not deeply embedded software; softer than Tier 5 typically implies
5. **Stripe level 1–2 admission softens Mason's timing** — the leading agentic commerce enabler says basic delegation not yet routine; bear case structurally sound but timing slow

### Triangulation Across Sources
Three of four sources (Mason, Visa VP, Stripe) describe an architecture where the network is NOT the obvious capture point for new rents. Only the MA Director places the network at the center — and his framing is the most defensive of the four. This asymmetry is the load-bearing element of the bear case.
