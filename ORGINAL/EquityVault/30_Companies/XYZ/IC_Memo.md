## Summary

Block cut 40% of its workforce in Q4 2025 and declared itself "intelligence-native." EBIT is accelerating. The AI narrative is credible — our APK teardown of Cash App reveals a coherent four-layer agentic commerce stack (Moneybot, Weaver, Fillr, CDP) built entirely in-house, and the internal orchestration layer (Goose) is production-grade across 26 revenue streams. The 40% RIF was enabled by a specific model capability (Claude Code, December 2024 vintage) that management named on our call. This is a real technology transformation, and the market is rewarding it.

The problem is what's underneath the acceleration. 100% of incremental Cash App gross profit growth is credit-led. Borrow went from zero to a $3.4 billion loan book in one year, originating $185 loans at 80% effective APRs to borrowers who are ~70% sub-585 FICO. The average borrower draws 17.4 times per year — this is a perpetual recycler plugging a structural $200 shortfall, not a credit product solving a problem. Loan losses grew 359% year-over-year in 2025. Real free cash flow swung from +$2 billion to negative as the loan book consumed cash. The Street's $2.4 billion FCF estimate for 2026 mirrors management guidance but does not properly account for the balance sheet intensity of a growing lending operation.

The description the market is applying — "AI-driven fintech with accelerating profitability" — is incomplete. The more accurate description is "subprime lender with a Utah ILC charter, wrapped in a genuinely impressive technology stack, whose headline growth is a function of credit expansion into an untested borrower cohort." Both descriptions contain truth. The stock works as long as the first one dominates. It breaks when the second one surfaces.

At 13.5x 2027 and 10x 2028 GAAP-ish earnings, the shares can work higher near-term on the accelerating headline and the AI narrative. I don't have a structural reason to own this. Neutral.

## Our Thesis

Block's headline growth is credit-led and untested in a downturn, but the technology platform — the only closed-loop ecosystem owning merchant POS, consumer wallet, payment rails, and AI orchestration — is genuinely differentiated and may be underpriced if Neighborhoods and the PBA conversion succeed. The stock is a trade on macro duration, not a compounder we hold through a cycle.

## The Business and How It Grows

### Cash App — 57% of Gross Profit

Cash App's 59 million monthly actives generate revenue across four products: peer-to-peer transfers and instant deposit (the original habit anchor), Cash Card interchange (84% gross margin, Durbin-exempt at ~1.6% vs. 0.21% for large banks), Borrow (88% GP margin, $185 average loan, 21-day duration), and Afterpay BNPL. Bitcoin revenue is large ($3.3 billion in 2025) but contributes minimal gross profit (~2% margin) and is economically irrelevant.

The growth engine has shifted decisively toward credit. From 2022 to 2025, Financial Solutions (Borrow + BNPL) drove 100% of incremental Cash App GP growth. Borrow originated $9 billion cumulatively through 2025, with the loan book reaching $3.4 billion after going from zero to on-balance-sheet at Square Financial Services (SFS) in nine months.

**The Utah ILC charter is the enabling mechanism.** SFS is a Utah-chartered industrial loan bank, FDIC-insured. Utah has no APR lending cap. Federal banking law allows SFS to export Utah's rate nationwide. The geographic expansion from 35 to 48 states was not about licensing — it was about moving origination in-house where federal preemption overrides state usury caps. In New York, Afterpay through its partner bank (First Electronic Bank) can only charge 25% APR matching the state cap. Borrow through SFS charges the standard 5% flat fee, translating to 80% APR. Expert calls confirmed Block had difficulty finding capital partners for these loans — unsurprising given the credit profile — which is why the ILC charter became necessary.

**The perpetual recycler dynamic.** The average Borrow user draws 17.4 times per year. A typical recycler pays ~$160 in annual fees on a balance that rarely exceeds $200. The borrower has a structural shortfall, not a one-time emergency. They borrow to bridge to payday, repay via auto-deduction from their deposit (Block has first right of refusal on incoming funds), and immediately borrow again because the repayment recreated the hole. This is extractive by design — it monetizes a permanent liquidity deficit without resolving it.

**The reported GP overstates the economics.** Loan losses sit in operating expenses, not as a deduction from revenue. Burdening GP with associated credit losses, the Cash App GP growth rate approximately halves — from ~30% reported to ~15% adjusted in 2025. Total Cash App losses grew 142% year-over-year; Borrow losses specifically grew 359%.

**The FCF problem.** Block's reported 2024 FCF was ~$2 billion. In 2025, real FCF swung negative. The mechanics: loan loss provisions add back to CFO (non-cash), but the actual cash drain shows up in CFI as net loan originations ($3.6 billion gross, partially offset by warehouse facility draws). The Street's 2026 FCF estimate of $2.4 billion mirrors management guidance and assumes the step-change was one-time. That may be right — the initial balance sheet build is the largest cash drag — but GP growth requires perpetual origination growth. There is no recurring revenue without recurring lending.

### Square (Seller) — 37% of Gross Profit

Square is a mature, mid-single-digit grower providing the merchant-side infrastructure: POS hardware, payment processing, payroll, invoicing, banking, and the new Neighborhoods closed-loop commerce product. Square GPV was approximately $60 billion in Q4 2025 annualized. The segment provides stability but limited excitement — take rates are under secular pressure from competition (Toast, Stripe, Clover) and Square's own decision to move upmarket toward mid-market sellers.

**Neighborhoods is the strategic wildcard.** Launched October 2025 for F&B merchants, it connects Square POS merchants with Cash App consumers through branded storefronts, online ordering, and Local Cash rewards (10% of subtotal, redeemable at any participating merchant). The headline economics: a 1% processing fee versus Square's standard 2.6% + $0.10, viable only when the consumer pays on Block's own rails (Cash App balance, Cash Card, or USDC stablecoin). Block subsidized 100% of Local Cash through January 20, 2026, then switched eligible F&B merchants from opt-in to opt-out.

Neighborhoods is the most credible path to un-inverting the funnel. A consumer who discovers a local coffee shop on Cash App, earns Local Cash, and routes spend through the Card is building a deposit-led relationship — exactly what Block lacks. The closed-loop competitive moat is real: Block is the only company that simultaneously owns the merchant POS, consumer wallet, payment rails, and cross-merchant reward currency. DoorDash has no POS or payment instrument. Toast has the POS but no consumer wallet. At ~4.25% fully loaded merchant cost, Neighborhoods undercuts DoorDash pickup commissions (6-15%) while letting the merchant keep the customer relationship.

The structural question is density. The product requires sufficient Square merchant × Cash App user overlap within a given neighborhood to generate local network effects. Q1 2026 earnings is the first real data point post-subsidy.

### The Technology Layer

The 40% RIF was not a blunt cost cut. Our APK teardown of Cash App v5.42.1 reveals a production-grade agentic commerce stack built entirely in-house:

**Moneybot** is a function-calling agent with persistent memory, extended reasoning, and generative native UI. It routes to any of Cash App's 100+ screens via a single `launch_flow` tool — meaning each new product (stablecoin, credit score, bills) is automatically accessible without incremental AI integration. The capability surface grows with the product surface. It is gated behind two Amplitude experiments and not yet generally available.

**Weaver** is a proactive recommendation engine running in the background, refreshing recommendations without user initiation and surfacing them as Money Tab tiles. It feeds from the proprietary CDP (customer data platform) built in-house rather than on Segment or Amplitude — behavioral signal stays proprietary.

**Fillr** provides checkout execution with cart extraction from merchant web pages. Walmart is explicitly managed via a named feature flag. **Draft Payments** stage transactions for human review before execution — the "prepare but don't fire" state required for agent-initiated commerce.

**Goose**, the internal AI orchestration layer, sits above frontier LLMs and deploys purpose-built agents (CFO agent, sales agent, compliance agent) across 26 revenue streams. IR confirmed it is model-agnostic by design but named Claude Code specifically as the capability that gave management conviction on the RIF. Token cost is in the tens of millions, included in 2026 guidance.

**Block is building its own payment protocol stack rather than adopting ACP, UCP, or AP2.** No external protocol references exist in the binary. MRI (Mobile Runtime Integrity) is Block's proprietary cryptographic attestation — architecturally identical to Google's AP2 but with Block controlling the attestation root. Stablecoin URIs and Pay Links provide agent-callable payment addresses without Stripe or OpenAI in the trust chain. This is consistent with Dorsey's public stance on open monetary protocols. The risk: ACP and UCP already have merchant adoption (Walmart, Etsy, Instacart on ACP; Shopify, Target, Wayfair on UCP). Block's alternative rail is consumer-side infrastructure without a matching merchant network today.

### The PBA Conversion — The Strategic Imperative

Growing Primary Banking Actives is the single most important initiative in 2026. PBAs generate nearly 10x the gross profit of P2P-only users: $10,500-$11,000 in annual card spend, ~19 transactions per month (vs. 6x for non-DD users), and 228 annual data points for underwriting precision. Cash App Green is the behavioral gating mechanism — spend $500/month on the Card or route direct deposit to unlock higher Borrow limits, priority support, Boost offers, and Moneybot access. Expert research indicates Green actives are 3x more likely to engage with Moneybot, and 70%+ selected proactive financial prompts.

Green is a brilliant retention tool for existing Cash App users. Whether it can acquire users already embedded in SoFi (national bank features, joint accounts, savings vaults), Robinhood Gold (3% IRA match, 3% credit card cashback, $324 billion in platform assets), or Chime (13% share of new checking accounts, fee-free $500 advances) is the open question.

### Management and Capital Allocation

Dorsey's shareholder letters tell a story of a description that changes every year while the credit engine underneath does not. 3Q21: "economic empowerment." 4Q22: "disciplined." 4Q23: "bank our base." 4Q24: "neighborhood network." 4Q25: "intelligence-native." Meanwhile, Borrow gets a single passing mention in 4Q23, one sentence about $9 billion in originations in 4Q24, and zero mentions in the 4Q25 letter that announced the 40% RIF. The gap between the narrative and the growth engine is worth noting.

SBC is material. GAAP earnings require SBC expensing; the non-GAAP headline meaningfully overstates cash economics. Bitcoin holdings add volatility to the income statement through mark-to-market adjustments that should be stripped from any operating analysis.

### Valuation

At 13.5x 2027 and 10x 2028 GAAP-ish EPS (SBC expensed, one-off severance excluded), the stock is priced for the acceleration to continue. The multiple is not demanding for a company growing EBIT at 20%+. The question is durability: if the credit-adjusted GP growth rate is 15% rather than 30%, and if the balance sheet intensity of Borrow consumes a meaningful portion of reported FCF, the valuation needs to compress or the growth needs to find a non-credit source. Neighborhoods and PBA conversion are that source, but neither has produced measurable results yet.

The description matters. If the market continues to describe Block as "AI-native fintech," the multiple holds or expands. If the market re-describes Block as "subprime consumer lender with a tech wrapper," the stock trades at 8-10x earnings — the historical range for specialty finance companies. The distance between 13.5x and 8x is 40% downside on no change in fundamentals.

## What Could Go Wrong

**Credit cycle.** ~70% sub-585 FICO, untested in recession. A single missed paycheck breaks the perpetual recycler cycle for the entire cohort. Consumer finance stocks have extremely high beta to credit deterioration. The 359% year-over-year growth in Borrow losses is occurring in a benign macro environment. The white-collar employment disruption theme (see 40_Themes) creates an unusual vector: if AI displaces the demographic that services lower-income consumers (retail managers, call center supervisors), the credit losses surface from an unexpected direction.

**Regulatory.** The Utah ILC charter enabling 80% APR nationwide is a regulatory end-run. CFPB is currently dormant, but state AGs and a future administration could revisit. If federal preemption is challenged, Borrow's geographic reach contracts from 48 states and the growth algorithm breaks.

**Neighborhoods fails to achieve density.** If the local network effects don't materialize post-subsidy, Block has no credible path to un-inverting the funnel. The credit-first model becomes the permanent model, and the stock deserves a consumer finance multiple.

**Agentic commerce protocol adoption.** Block is building a proprietary payment stack while ACP and UCP gain merchant adoption. If ACP becomes the standard and Block's MRI/stablecoin rail remains consumer-only, the closed-loop thesis narrows to the physical world at Square POS — a smaller surface than the full agentic commerce opportunity.

---

## Conclusion

Neutral. The technology is genuinely differentiated — the agentic commerce stack, the Goose orchestration layer, and the closed-loop Neighborhoods architecture are assets that no competitor can replicate in the near term. The 40% RIF and AI-forward posture are credible operational transformations. The stock works in the near term on accelerating headline growth and a favorable description.

The credit mechanics underneath are extractive, untested, and consuming cash. The gap between reported and credit-adjusted GP is the single most important analytical point on this name. Until Neighborhoods produces measurable results or PBA conversion demonstrates that Block can grow without deepening subprime credit exposure, the growth is fragile in a way the multiple does not reflect.

We would upgrade to Buy on: (1) Neighborhoods disclosure showing meaningful GPV contribution and merchant retention post-subsidy, (2) credit-adjusted GP growth accelerating to 20%+ without balance sheet intensity increasing, or (3) a price correction to 8-10x forward earnings that compensates for the credit risk. We would move to Avoid on: rising Borrow delinquencies in a macro slowdown, regulatory action against the ILC charter, or management accelerating originations into deteriorating credit quality.
