## 2026-04-29 — F3Q26 Earnings: Beat-and-Raise, Stock Down ~3.5%

**Source:** MSFT_F3Q_2026_Call_Notes.docx

### Headline
Revenue $82.9B (+18% / +15% CC). Cleared the buyside whisper bar on Azure. Copilot inflecting. OpenAI deal recut. But the win is incremental, not step-function — stock down ~3.5% into the print.

### Azure: The Number That Matters
- F4Q guide: **39-40% CC** — vs. Klein's "needs 38%+ to RIP" framing
- First explicit guide to "modest acceleration in 2H calendar year vs. 1H"
- Intelligent Cloud F4Q guide: $37.95-38.25B (+27-28%)
- Demand still exceeds supply; "constrained at least through 2026"

### Copilot: Bear Case Dismantled
- Paid M365 Copilot seats: **>20M** (vs. ~15M last quarter)
- Seat adds: **+250% YoY** — fastest growth since launch
- Customers >50K seats: **quadrupled YoY**
- Accenture: 740K seats (largest deal); Bayer, J&J, Mercedes, Roche: 90K+ each
- F4Q net seat adds expected to increase sequentially
- Weekly engagement at "same level as Outlook" — daily habit, not pilot

### Capex: Going Way Up
- F4Q capex: **>$40B** (includes ~$5B higher component pricing)
- CY26 capex: **~$190B** (includes ~$25B higher component pricing)
- Same "higher component pricing" language META used — industry-wide GPU/memory cost issue
- Mix: ~⅔ short-lived (GPU/CPU), ⅓ long-lived (15+ year assets)
- FY27: "another year of double-digit revenue and operating income growth" — reaffirms but doesn't quantify capex

### OpenAI Recut
- Revenue share extended through 2030 (predictability)
- IP royalty-free through 2032
- Elimination of MSFT's rev share to OpenAI
- **Net: multi-year IP optionality without rev share burden**

### The Agentic Strategic Reframe
- Nadella: "agents will become the dominant workload"
- Business model: per-seat → "seat plus consumption" (license + meter)
- GitHub Copilot moving to usage-based pricing June 1
- ~60% of customers already on usage-based credits
- Copilot credit consumption up **~2x QoQ**
- Agent 365: tens of millions of agents already managed

### AI Margin Defense — Concrete Proof Points
- Maia 200 accelerator: **+30% better tokens/dollar** vs. latest fleet silicon (live in Iowa/Arizona)
- 40% improvement in inference throughput for most-used Copilot models
- Whisper: **+67% GPU efficiency**
- MAI Image 2: **+260% efficiency**
- GPU dock lifetimes reduced **~20% YTD**
- Hood on record: "AI margins are structurally better than the cloud transition was"

### RPO Mechanics
- Commercial RPO ex-OpenAI: **$627B** (+99% YoY, ~2.5yr weighted duration)
- 25% recognizes in next 12 months (+39% YoY)
- Portion beyond 12 months: **+138%**
- Bookings ex-OpenAI: +7% (vs. reported -4%; OAI distortion noted)

### Headcount and Margin Discipline
- Total company headcount: **declined YoY** in Q3
- F4Q includes ~$900M voluntary retirement program (one-time: ~$350M COGS, ~$550M opex)
- FY26 full-year op margin: **up ~1pt YoY** (including $900M one-times)
- FY27: headcount declines again; opex growth mid-to-high single digits

### What Analysts Pushed Hardest On
1. **"Who pays for all this?"** (Weiss, Morgan Stanley) — capex/IT spend ceiling question → answered with seat-to-consumption reframe
2. **Capex execution** (Keirstead, UBS) — $120B implied 2H pickup → "feel quite good," leaning short-lived assets
3. **AI margins** (Thill, Jefferies) — Hood: structurally better than cloud transition
4. **Capex vs. revenue disconnect** (Mortlock, Bernstein) — pivoted to short-lived assets correlating with revenue + $600B+ RPO
5. **Copilot product evolution** (Borges, Goldman) — chat → agents → edit mode → delegation; multi-model ("I generate using Opus and check with Codex")
6. **Seat vs. consumption** (Jaluria, RBC) — challenged "everything goes consumption" given E7 still seat-anchored

### Open Questions
- Is $190B CY26 capex the peak or the new normal?
- How quickly does consumption cannibalize/complement seat revenue?
- When does Maia 200 reach sufficient scale to materially bend the GPU cost curve?
- Will RPO conversion rates hold at current pace?
