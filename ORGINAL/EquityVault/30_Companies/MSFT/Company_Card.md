## One-Liner
Microsoft cleared the buyside whisper bar on Azure (39-40% CC F4Q guide), disclosed Copilot accelerating dramatically (>20M seats, +250% adds YoY), and recut the OpenAI deal in their favor — but the stock is down ~3.5% because the win is incremental, not a step-function reset, and $190B CY26 capex (including $25B from component cost inflation) raises "who pays for all this?" concerns.

## Bull Case
1. **Azure demand exceeds supply** — First explicit guide to "modest acceleration in 2H calendar year vs. 1H"; constrained through 2026.
2. **Copilot inflection confirmed** — >20M seats; +250% YoY adds (fastest since launch); customers >50K seats quadrupled YoY; daily habit engagement.
3. **Seat-to-consumption model transition** — Business model shifting from per-seat to "seat plus consumption"; GitHub Copilot moving usage-based June 1; credit consumption up ~2x QoQ.
4. **OpenAI recut** — Revenue share extended through 2030; IP royalty-free through 2032; elimination of MSFT's rev share to OpenAI.
5. **AI margin defense** — Maia 200: +30% better tokens/dollar vs. latest fleet; Whisper +67% GPU efficiency; MAI Image 2 +260%; GPU dock lifetimes -20% YTD.
6. **RPO $627B** — +99% YoY (ex-OpenAI); 25% recognizes in 12 months (+39% YoY); portion beyond 12 months +138%.
7. **Margin discipline** — Headcount declined YoY; FY26 op margin up ~1pt; FY27: headcount declines again, opex growth mid-to-high single digits.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Capex intensity** — F4Q >$40B; CY26 ~$190B including $25B from higher component pricing; "who pays for all this?" question from Morgan Stanley's Keith Weiss.
2. **Component cost inflation** — $25B industry-wide GPU/memory cost issue (same language as META); not company-specific but material.
3. **Incremental, not step-function** — Stock down ~3.5% on beat-and-raise; the market wants a bigger inflection signal.
4. **Capex vs. revenue disconnect** — Implied 2H capex pickup significant; whether returns match investment scale is unproven.
5. **Consumption model risk** — Transitioning from predictable seats to variable consumption; harder to model, could disappoint.

## Key Monitoring
1. Azure CC growth trajectory (F4Q guide 39-40%; need to see sustained acceleration in 2H CY26).
2. Copilot net seat adds (expected to increase sequentially in F4Q).
3. CY26 capex actuals vs. ~$190B guide — volume vs. price decomposition.
4. Consumption revenue mix shift (credit consumption growth, GitHub usage-based uptake).
5. FY27 capex guidance when provided (currently "another year of double-digit growth" — no capex specifics).
6. AI margin metrics (tokens/dollar, GPU efficiency gains, Maia 200 deployment scale).
