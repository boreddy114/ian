## February 2026 — IC Framework: Classified as "Avoid" (Subsequently Overturned)

**Source:** Scarcity Straddlers Physical Tethers IC Memo.docx

### Original Classification
MCO was classified under Zone 3 (Abundance / Avoid) in the IC memo's initial framework:

> "Financial services 'information services': Moody's, S&P Global, Verisk, FICO, Equifax, TransUnion, Guidewire, MSCI — these are software businesses with financial data."

> "If it is pure software — regardless of what industry vertical it serves — it is in the abundance zone and we do not own it."

### What Changed
The Catalini Moat Screen (April 2026) demonstrated that this classification was a category error:
1. MCO is not pure software — it is a regulatory-coordination-equilibrium business.
2. 40–50% of MA profit is ratings-derivative, inheriting MIS's regulatory protection.
3. The NRSRO coordination equilibrium is structurally unforkable — unlike payments, where regulation-neutral infrastructure enabled disruption.
4. Practitioners explicitly distinguish regulatory-embedded vendors from commercial software vendors.

This finding led to the creation of the "Verification-Tethered Software" category in the physical tether framework.
