## 2026-04-15 — Catalini Moat Screen: Initial Assessment

**Source:** Snowflake Catalini Moat Screen April 2026.docx + Catalini Moat Screen Snowflake SNOW.docx
**Rating:** Under Review

### Assessment: CONTESTED (borderline Vulnerable)
- Weighted Classical: 3.1-3.3 / 5.0
- Weighted Agentic Vulnerability: 3.0-3.3 / 5.0
- Switching Cost Bifurcation: ~65-75% Category A / ~25-35% Category B

### Core Insight
Snowflake has strong classical moats rooted in data gravity and consumption-model switching costs, but elevated agentic vulnerability. The majority of switching cost is automatable migration labor, not coordination or compliance. The market prices data gravity at infrastructure-grade durability; the framework says it's more vulnerable.

### Three Material Moats
1. **System of Record (Primary, Classical 3.4-3.8, Agentic 3.3):** Analytical SoR with petabytes, hundreds of pipelines. NOT operational SoR. Depth primarily in execution infrastructure, not compliance.
2. **Ground Truth / Data (Secondary, Classical 3.2, Agentic 3.5):** Data gravity from volume/complexity. Snowflake does NOT own proprietary data. Overwhelmingly execution-grade, not verification-grade.
3. **Network Effects (Secondary, Classical 2.5-3.0, Agentic 3.0-3.2):** Marketplace (2,700+ listings) + data sharing. Indirect complementary network. Real but shallow vs. combinatorial networks.

### Keystone Moat: Data gravity (SoR switching cost). If migration tools crack this, entire chain weakens.
### Backstop Moat: Compliance lock-in in regulated industries.
### Critical Vulnerability: Iceberg (open table formats) as single-point failure that cascades across all three moat types.

### 5-Year Trajectory
"Roughly the same or modestly narrower because data gravity switching cost is being structurally eroded by open table formats, agent-powered migration tools, and Databricks' push into core warehousing."
