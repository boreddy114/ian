Sector Context
The capital goods sector is heterogenous, spanning 15 industries and 30 end markets. To generalise, the sector is exposed to three distinct cycle groups: (1) industrial short-cycle, where industries such as bearings, automation, cutting tools and compressors all have shorter lead times, largely sell through distributors, follow similar growth paths in each region, are highly cyclical and economically sensitive, (2) medium-cycle building related industries, where industries such as elevators, locks, low voltage and lighting are somewhat less cyclical and follow residential and non-residential construction trends and (3) long-cycle capex exposed industries such as equipment for rail, mining, oil, marine, power and food & beverage capex.
In general, our current investment strategy is not to chose early vs late cycle, or vice versa, but to favour those companies in all three cyclical buckets that are the most favourably exposed to industries facing attractive secular growth themes which we believe are likely to lift through-cycle growth. We see four themes (1) energy efficiency including electrification, EV, battery, grid reinforcement, autonomous buildings, trains, drives, heat exchangers , highly efficient motors and compressors), (2) digitalisation, including automation, PLM software, metaverse, edge devices, 5G, generative AI and data centres, (3) re-shoring & near-shoring predominantly automation and massive government spending programmes (IRA, IIJA, CHIPS, EC green deal).
Timing is always hard but after a period of destocking, we believe that further destocking pain likely to be limited and short-cycle orders should bottom before the year-end. With backlogs set to remain elevated through 2024, the consumer in better shape than feared, corporates in a strong position to spend and valuation not overly stretched, we maintain our positive stance.
