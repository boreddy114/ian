## One-Liner
Xylem is the only vertically integrated pure-play on water infrastructure — pumps, metering, treatment, and digital analytics across the full water lifecycle — entering a multi-year margin expansion cycle via 80/20 discipline with emerging data center cooling optionality, but the earnings algorithm hasn't yet caught up to the franchise quality at ~22x forward.

## Bull Case
1. **Thermal management bottleneck = structural water demand.** AI rack densities >100kW require liquid cooling; once liquid is mandatory, heat rejection is inseparable from water availability. XYL is the water-movement-and-control leg of the thermal management thesis.
2. **80/20 margin expansion cycle.** New CFO from IDEX (Danaher-lineage 80/20 expert). Guiding 300bps EBIT margin expansion 2025–2027 from 20.5% base. JCI analogy: similar journey produced multiple re-rating from high-teens to low-20s P/E.
3. **Data center exposure inflecting.** Applied Water 1Q26 DC orders alone exceeded all of FY25 — first time data centers named as segment-moving driver. Graduated from "thematically adjacent" to "in the segment-driver script."
4. **BOO capability proven at scale.** $850M, 20-year outsourced water contract (largest in company history) validates Evoqua thesis. Same capability set hyperscalers will want for liquid-cooled AI infrastructure.
5. **Defensive revenue base.** ~45% public utilities (non-discretionary replacement + regulatory compliance), ~45% industrial (semiconductor, pharma — own ESG mandates). Low-volatility, through-cycle demand.
6. **Only ~1% of global freshwater is accessible for human use.** ~30% of treated water lost to leakage. Enormous, underpenetrated secular market.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Earnings algorithm not yet delivering.** 1Q26: 0% organic growth, FCF ~88% below consensus, $38M restructuring charges (up 41% YoY). Back-half-loaded guidance requires multiple things working simultaneously.
2. **Recurring "one-time" charges.** Adj. vs. GAAP gap of $0.33/qtr. Restructuring charges have been recurring for over a decade. Northcoast refuses to acknowledge adjusted reporting.
3. **Hockey-stick 2H ramp risk.** FY guide requires 2H organic growth of 4–6% — depends on MCS meter conversion, WI lapping walkaway, Applied Water volume normalization, all landing together.
4. **Valuation rich for current delivery.** ~22x adj. forward on 0% organic and FCF-light quarter. More comfortable at ~18–19x ($100–105).
5. **China deterioration.** Revenue down ~30% YoY (⅓ market, ⅓ competitive, ⅓ self-directed walkaway). <3% of revenue but a growth headwind.
6. **Smart metering lumpiness.** Large municipal deployments subject to budget timing and project delays. H2 recovery is planning assumption, not contracted backlog.

## Valuation
At $123.51, XYL trades at ~22x adj. forward EPS of ~$5.56 (JPMe FY26). Northcoast GAAP basis: ~31x on $4.05. JPM PT $140 implies 15x FY27E EV/EBITDA (2 turns below 5-year average). Franchise quality justifies premium but algorithm isn't delivering yet.

**Entry target: ~$100–105 (~18–19x adj. forward)** — franchise quality and thematic optionality compensate for 0% organic and cash-quality drag at that level.

## Catalyst Path
1. **2Q26 print (late July):** Cleanest near-term signal — does 1H FCF normalize? Does MCS BTB recover >1? Does management quantify DC exposure?
2. **Applied Water DC order conversion:** Revenue should follow BTB >1; if management gives a sized DC number, thesis advances from "plausible" to "in motion."
3. **WSS pipeline development:** More BOO deals like the $850M landmark contract — validates portability to hyperscaler water infrastructure.
4. **80/20 margin inflection:** Quarter where restructuring charges drop materially toward zero — establishes credibility of "normalized" earnings power.
5. **Smart metering project conversion:** H2 2026 recovery in MCS confirms pipeline is timing, not cancellation.
