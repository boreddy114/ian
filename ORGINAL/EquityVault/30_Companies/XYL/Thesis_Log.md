## March 2026 — Research Note: Water Infrastructure & 80/20 Margin Cycle

**Source:** XYL_Research_Note.docx

### Thesis

Xylem is entering a multi-year margin expansion cycle underpinned by 80/20 operational discipline, rational capital allocation, and structural demand from an industry that remains dramatically underinvested in efficiency. Revenue growth will be below trend in 2026 due to deliberate portfolio pruning and smart metering lumpiness — this creates a better entry point.

### Key Arguments

**Water is the structural backdrop.** Only ~1% of global freshwater is accessible for human use. ~30% of treated water lost to leakage before reaching end users. Enormous, underpenetrated secular market.

**Only vertically integrated pure-play.** XYL handles clean and dirty water pumping, metering, leak detection, services, bound together with a digital analytics platform. Full-stack positioning is a genuine differentiator.

**Phase 2: Margin expansion.** Patrick Decker's decade assembled the portfolio (Phase 1). Handoff to Matthew Pine + new CFO from IDEX (deep 80/20 experience) = Phase 2: extracting returns. Guiding 300bps EBIT margin expansion 2025–2027 from 20.5% base.

**JCI analogue.** JCI under a Danaher-lineage CEO: 600bps margin improvement commitment → multiple re-rated from high-teens to low-20s P/E. Xylem targeting 300–400bps, earlier in cycle.

## March 2026 — Thermal Management Thematic: The Next Scarcity Regime

**Source:** ACTIVE_Thermal Management.docx

### Thesis

AI workloads generate heat densities exceeding the physical limits of air cooling. Liquid cooling is mandatory at >100kW/rack. Once liquid is mandatory, heat rejection becomes inseparable from water availability, movement, and quality. Water is geographically fixed, politically sensitive, and increasingly regulated — a zero-sum local game where water availability, not capital, determines time-to-market.

### The Water Nexus — Three Interlocking Constraints

| Constraint | Description | Key Data Points |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct On-Site (Scope 1)** | Evaporative cooling at hyperscale = millions of gallons/day per campus. Competes with households for treated, potable municipal supply. | Microsoft water consumption rising 11x from '24–'28. Google fleet: 7.7B gallons potable water in 2024. |
| **Indirect Power (Scope 2)** | Thermal power plants are largest industrial water users. Heatwaves/droughts curtail generation. DC fails because upstream plant can't legally operate. | Phoenix DC cluster could lift regional water stress 32%. |
| **Semiconductor Mfg (Scope 3)** | Leading-edge fabs consume tens of millions of gallons/day of ultrapure water. 2nm nodes increase intensity. | TSMC: 129M m³ in 2024 (+13% YoY). 161L per 12-inch equivalent wafer mask layer. |

### Industry Solutions

1. **Closed-loop DTC cooling** — eliminates freshwater supply; 30–50% CAPEX premium justified by bypassing multi-year water permitting.
2. **Immersion cooling** — zero onsite water; highest cost but functions as location unlock.
3. **Software-defined thermal optimization** — extends operating envelopes, improves economics.

### XYL's Position
XYL is the **water-movement-and-control leg** of the thermal management thesis: pumps, valves, leak detection, digital controls for compliance with increasingly stringent water constraints. ~100% of revenue tied to water infrastructure theme, though data center-specific work currently a small portion.
