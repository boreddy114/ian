### Key Findings

**Gas Power — Demand Acceleration**
- 100 GW under contract (44 GW backlog + 56 GW SRAs), up from 83 GW
- Q1: 21 GW signed; year-end target ≥110 GW
- 25 gas turbines shipped (+32% YoY); on track for 20 GW annualized output by Q3 2026
- Pricing: 1H 2026 orders priced 10-20 pts higher $/kW vs. Q4 2025
- Customer mix: ~80% traditional utilities, ~20% data centers
- 231 HA units on order, >100 not yet commissioned
- Lead time: ~3 years; only ~10 GW remaining cumulative for 2029-2030

**Electrification — Inflection Point**
- Orders +86% to ~$7.1B; data center orders $2.4B (exceeded all of 2025)
- Revenue +61% GAAP / +29% organic; EBITDA margin 17.8% (+590 bps)
- Backlog: $42B (vs. $9B at YE 2022)
- Prolec contributed ~$500M at >20% EBITDA margin; backlog $5B (+25% since announcement)
- HVDC backlog: ~$10B
- First EMS data center order signals $200-300M+ entitlement per GW
- TAM at end of decade: ~$300B addressable

**Wind — Still Bleeding**
- Orders +85% (cyclical bounce), revenue -25%, EBITDA loss -$382M
- FY26 guide unchanged: down low-double digits, ~$400M EBIT loss
- Only 30% of 2026 onshore turbine shipments in 1H
- Offshore: Dogger Bank B & C completion through 2027

**Capital Allocation**
- Prolec acquisition: $5.3B (remaining 50% stake)
- Dispositions: ~$900M (manufacturing software, China XD)
- Shareholder returns: ~$1.4B (dividends + $1.3B buybacks)
- Debt issuance: $2.6B; remains <1x gross debt/EBITDA
- R&D up ~25%, largest portion in Electrification
- New products: solid-state transformer delivering Fall 2026; MV UPS; EMS

- 26 AI process transformations in progress (doubled from 13)
