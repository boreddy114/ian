## April 21, 2026 — Dead Money Into 2026: Neutral, Trim on Strength

**Source:** DRAFT WRB Dead Money Into 2026.docx

### Thesis Tracker

| Item | What We're Watching | Status |
|---|---|---|
| 2026 top-line growth | Q4 printed +2% NPW; management implied improvement but declined to guide. Sub-5% growth breaks the premium-multiple narrative. | Initial |
| Underlying loss ratio trajectory | Berkley eased off pushing rate in select casualty lines. 50-100bps deterioration in 2026 makes the premium multiple look stretched. | Initial |
| Relative valuation vs. RNR, TRV, AON | 14.9x 2026 P/E is ~36% premium to TRV, ~20% to ACGL. Watch whether RNR reserve-driven BV growth draws capital away. | Initial |

### What Happened
Cantor downgraded WRB from OW to Neutral (Apr 8), cutting PT from $75 to $71 (2.3x YE26 BVPS, 14x 2027 EPS). The original OW thesis — WRB wrongly bucketed as pure E&S — has played out. Broader sell-side moving the same direction: UBS, Barclays (Sell), Evercore (Sell), KBW, MS, BofA all trimmed PTs. Stock at ~$66, down from ~$74 at YE25, near 52-week lows, below 200-day MA.

### Key Findings

**The cycle is turning but WRB is not the extreme expression of it**
- BofA cross-sectional data shows WRB's 2025 combined ratio of 90.7% is only 230bps inside its 20-year average and 180bps inside its 10-year
- Real mean-reversion exposure sits with TRV (-1,430bps vs. 20yr), ALL Protection (-980bps), and personal auto writers
- WRB runs closer to through-cycle level — consistency and base hits, not hard-market windfalls

**The premium multiple is the problem, not the underwriting**
- 14.9x 2026E P/E = ~36% premium to TRV (10.9x), meaningful premium to CB (13.7x), HIG (10.5x), ACGL (~10x)
- Premium was defensible when WRB was compounding BV at 20%+ on high-single-digit organic growth with expanding margins
- In a world of low-single-digit growth, 50-100bps margin compression, and -19% cat treaty, the premium has to compress

**Reserve redundancy is the real defense**
- Q4 commentary on favorable development across longer-tail lines is the best argument for staying long
- Same setup as RNR — operators with real reserve cushion can hold calendar-year C/R even as underlying deteriorates
- If 2026 prints 2-3 quarters of material favorable PYD, calendar-year ROE stays north of 20%

**Relative value argument, not absolute derating**
- Capital in WRB at 14.9x for ~6% implied upside vs. RNR at 1.2x BV with a year's worth of earnings in reserve redundancy, or AON at 28% implied upside
- WRB is a relative-value trim, not a short

### Rating & Action
- **Rating:** Neutral. Trim on strength, do not add.
- **Upgrade conditions:** Two consecutive quarters of flat/improving underlying loss ratio with NPW growth returning to 5%+, or material capital return event closing valuation gap
- **Downgrade conditions:** Commercial auto underlying LR deterioration >100bps, reinsurance C/R above 90%, or failure of favorable PYD to materialize in H1-26
- **Next decision point:** Q1-26 results (late April). Pair-trade: reduce WRB, add RNR.
