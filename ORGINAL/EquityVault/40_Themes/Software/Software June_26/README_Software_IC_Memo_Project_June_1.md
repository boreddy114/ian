## 1. What this project is

We are closing roughly half of a ~250bps software/services **underweight** by selling INTU and adding durability-gated names. Each name gets a full **IC memo** written with the **v2 IC memo skill** (`/mnt/skills/user/ic-memo-skill-v2/SKILL.md`). The memos hang off a single **house-view thesis doc** (the spine).

**Governing framework (settled):** Classical moats were implicitly *labor* moats. AI dissolves labor moats but NOT moats anchored in **verification, coordination, or physical/legal consequence**. The market sorts software on *AI-coupling* (does revenue track AI capex); we sort on *durability* (does the moat survive AI). The mispricing lives in that gap. Discipline rules: **"durable ≠ buy," "beaten-down ≠ cheap."** Durability is a non-negotiable gate; price is the second test.

**NEW (this chat) — and it now has teeth:** the durability gate and the price test are genuinely separable, and a name can pass the first and fail the second. **VEEV is the proof:** its moat cleared the durability gate decisively (pressure-test confirmed), yet it landed **Neutral** on price-and-growth. VEEV is now the project's worked example of *"durable but not yet buyable"* — the complement to SNOW, which is the *"not durable / what to avoid"* example.

## 4. HARD-WON STYLE RULES (do not regress)

1. **Institutional register, not retail.** State the decomposition; don't narrate the arithmetic to a beginner.
2. **EPS algorithm = a bridge TABLE** (Driver / Contribution / Nature), not a walk-through paragraph. Mandatory.
3. **Proof Points = the concerns map, each headed "[Concern]. My judgment: [our conclusion]."** Evidence is cited as SUPPORT for OUR judgment — never "the market worried X, an expert said Y." Our judgment is the content, not the experts'.
4. **Include where we DISAGREE or concede.** A memo that only amplifies the bull side is not analysis. (VEEV concedes heavily — that's why it's Neutral.)
5. **Override management spin where warranted.** (VEEV: overrode the CFO's implied guide-low-and-raise comfort — he himself tempered it; see §8.)
6. **NO contrastive negation** ("not X, but Y" / "the question is not A, it's B"). Banned by the skill; Ian flags it as an AI tell.
7. **No research-process narration.** Never "after pulling the transcript."
8. **Voice:** "I" for analytical judgments, "we" for firm actions/positioning.
9. **No sell-side analyst or firm names** in the memo body. Management names (CEO/CFO) are fine. (Competitor management at public conferences — e.g., Salesforce's HLS head — is fine, referenced generically.)
10. **Sources: primary only.** Blocked: Motley Fool, Seeking Alpha, Yahoo, StockAnalysis, GuruFocus, TipRanks, etc. SEC filings, IR releases, transcripts, user uploads, Quartr.

**NEW STANDING PRINCIPLE (this chat — now in Claude memory):** **"Revenue growth drives multiples."** A legitimate *structural* growth deceleration is a real problem for the stock. Do NOT underwrite multiple expansion until the market gets comfortable with where stable growth settles, or whether growth can reaccelerate. Treat re-rating as **gated optionality, not a base-case return driver**, when growth has stepped down. This principle is load-bearing in the VEEV Neutral and should be applied to every name (it is why "cheap" is not a catalyst).
