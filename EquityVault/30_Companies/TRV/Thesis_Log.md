## 2026-04-16 — 1Q26 Earnings: Pricing Transition Deepens, Margins Holding But Window Narrows

**Source:** ACTIVE_1Q26_Pricing_Transition_Deepens_v2.docx; DRAFT_TRV_1Q26_Headline_Flattered_By_Cats.docx; DRAFT_TRV_1Q26_Pricing_Transition_Deepens_v2.docx; 1Q26-Press-Release-FINAL.pdf; 1Q26-Webcast-FINAL.pdf; sell-side first takes (Jefferies, JPM, HSBC)
**Rating:** Hold (Neutral)

### Thesis Established

TRV is a best-in-class commercial P&C franchise navigating a seven-year pricing cycle transition. The thesis centers on: (1) BI renewal rates decelerating linearly toward loss cost trends; (2) the superior middle-market franchise defending margins better than large-account peers; and (3) excess capital return sustaining total returns through the soft phase. Hold for relative P&C outperformance during the pricing downturn.

YoY comparison wildly distorted by cat losses ($761M vs. $2,266M in 1Q25 from severe winter storms). The reported CR improvement of 13.9pts is almost entirely cats + PYD. The underlying CR actually deteriorated 50bps.

### Pricing Cycle Data

BI renewal rate change (ex-national accounts) continues its uninterrupted linear descent:

| Metric | 1Q25 | 2Q25 | 3Q25 | 4Q25 | 1Q26 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| BI Renewal Rate Chg. | 5.6% | 5.2% | 4.3% | 3.8% | 3.2% |
| Middle Market Rate Chg. | 7.2% | 6.8% | 6.2% | 5.1% | 4.3% |
| Select Accounts Rate Chg. | 5.6% | — | — | — | 4.0% |
| BI Retention | 86% | 85% | 85% | 85% | 86% |
| MM New Business ($M) | $437 | $430 | $389 | $393 | $468 |

Middle market renewal rate of 4.3% is now approaching the 3-5% range estimated for casualty loss cost inflation (social inflation, medical costs, construction costs). The gap between middle market (4.3%) and select accounts (4.0%) is narrowing — thesis that middle market defends margins better is still true but less differentiated.

Two offsets: (1) retention ticked up to 86%/89% — TRV is not buying retention with price cuts; (2) new business at $775M (BI) and $468M (MM) were both sequential highs — selective share gains alongside rate moderation.

Personal auto pricing effectively zeroed out at 0.6% RPC. TRV responding with discipline: PIF declining 3% YoY, lowest new business in 5 quarters. Homeowners RPC decelerated sharply (9.5% from 19.0%) but remains well above loss trends with outstanding 69.7% underlying CR.

### Underlying Margin Pressure

- Consolidated underlying CR: 85.3% (+50bps YoY)
- Business Insurance: 89.8% (+160bps)
- Bond & Specialty: 88.9% (+160bps)
- Personal Insurance: 78.3% (-160bps, driven by homeowners)
- Expense ratio: 29.0% vs. 28.5% FY guide (management attributes to Canada divestiture timing; JPM flagged as primary shortfall)
- Underlying loss ratio: 56.3% vs. 56.2% — essentially flat; core underwriting engine holding. Margin pressure is from the expense side as premiums decelerate.

### Reserve Development

Six consecutive quarters with >$300M favorable PYD. 1Q26: $413M from workers' comp and commercial property (BI), fidelity and surety (B&S), auto and home (PI). These are the "safe" short-tail releases. The question for future quarters: adequacy of longer-tail casualty reserves (general liability, commercial auto — social inflation exposed).

### Capital Return

$2.223B total returned vs. $1.696B core income (131% payout ratio). Sustainable near-term on $31.1B statutory capital, $2.4B holding company liquidity, 21.2% debt-to-capital, Aa3/AA- ratings. Trailing 4Q core ROE 22.7% well above mid-teens target. Dividend +14% to $1.25/share (22-year streak, 8% CAGR). Industry excess capital flowing to shareholders rather than underwriting capacity — itself a signal the cycle is turning.

### Valuation

At ~$299/share: ~1.85x adjusted book ($161.60), ~10x trailing core EPS. Sell-side uniformly cautious (Jefferies Hold/$321, JPM UW, HSBC Hold/$305). Normalized ROE framework (mid-teens ROE on growing book) → fair value $280-310. Currently trading roughly at fair value.
