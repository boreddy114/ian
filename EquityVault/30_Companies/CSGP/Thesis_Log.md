## April 2026 — Catalini Moat Screen: CONTESTED with Fortress Sleeve

### Composite Moat Map (Post-Pressure Test)

| Moat Type | Classical | Agentic Vuln. | Tier | Importance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SoR / Switching (Commercial) | 4.0 | 3.4 | 3–4 | PRIMARY |
| Ground Truth / Data (product-weighted) | 4.3 | 2.8 | Mixed* | PRIMARY |
| Network — Apartments.com | 4.2 | 1.8 | 5–6 | SECONDARY |
| Network — Homes.com | 1.7 | 3.3 | 1–2 | TERTIARY |
| Brand / Reference Standard | 3.5 | 2.5 | 4 | REINFORCEMENT |

### The Three-Moat Stack
1. **CoStar Commercial** (CONTESTED): Reference-standard data + Schelling-point coordination equilibrium for CRE. "100% of commercial real estate brokers use CoStar." CMBS underwriters use it "similar to how they use Moody's." But pricing power eroding near-term.

2. **Apartments.com** (FORTRESS): Genuine Tier 5–6 coordination equilibrium in US apartment listings. 99% monthly PM renewal. Standalone $10–15B value — the under-credited part of the story.

3. **Homes.com** (VULNERABLE): Attempted network build that is NOT winning the architectural battle. No exclusive listing deals (Zillow has four). $1B+ cumulative investment. Unit economics devastating.

### Market Pricing Gap
- **What the market prices:** Unitary "data-centric moat" for CRE, with Residential as a separate risk factor (Homes.com).
- **What the framework identifies:** Three distinct moat architectures at different vulnerability tiers.
- **Where the edge lives:** Apartments.com is NOT in the consensus moat narrative. The market bundles it into Residential and frames it as the Homes.com problem. Standalone, it may be worth most of the current EV.

### Thesis Kill Conditions
1. Apartments.com PM renewal drops below 95% for 2 consecutive quarters
2. Realized pricing-through-renewal below 3% for 2 quarters
3. AI-native CRE data competitor achieves credibility with major institutional customer ($100M+ funding + Tier-1 client)
