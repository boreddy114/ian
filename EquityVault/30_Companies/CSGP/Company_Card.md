## One-Liner
CSGP is a three-moat-stack the market conflates as one: a genuinely durable Commercial data moat (Tier 4–5 core, "like rebuilding Disney World"), an even-more-durable Apartments.com marketplace fortress (Tier 5–6, plausibly worth $10–15B standalone against $16.3B EV), and a structurally contested Homes.com build consuming capital to chase a coordination equilibrium that is actively tilting toward competitors. The cleanest edge is the sum-of-the-parts argument where Apartments.com alone may be worth most of the current market cap.

## Bull Case
1. **Apartments.com is a Tier 5–6 fortress the market isn't pricing.** 99% monthly PM renewal, 67% brand awareness vs. 36% #2, Zillow Rentals traffic -48% YoY. Standalone worth $10–15B at comparable marketplace multiples (25–35x EBITDA).
2. **Core Commercial moat is more durable than consensus credits.** 1,600 researchers, military-grade aerial drone fleet, 35M legally protected images, give-to-get private bank loan data network. "Like trying to rebuild Disney World."
3. **94% CoStar Suite gross renewal rate** at highest since Q4'22 with net new bookings +42% in Q4.
4. **Activist resolution creates bimodal optionality.** Third Point + D.E. Shaw pushing for Homes.com shutdown or 2027 break-even. "Two ways to win — either management succeeds or the activists succeed."
5. **Multiple compression overdone.** Market-implied CAP of ~3 years is at the low end of framework-expected 3–7 year range for a Contested/Fortress stack.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Pricing-power compression is happening NOW.** Avenue5 Residential stopped using CoStar for new-build construction in 2024, shifted to Altus Group. Triad canceling six-figure contract for Claude at $20/month. 4.5% annual escalators producing customer animosity.
2. **Multifamily Commercial Data is actively being replaced.** HelloData.ai at $200/month already replicating apartment data. AI-native scraping of public property websites is feasible TODAY.
3. **Homes.com unit economics are devastating.** $20M+ sales teams refusing $10K/month subs. Subscribed agents report "one single lead for the past 12 months." No exclusive listing deals.
4. **Homes.com profitability not until 2030** (management) or 2027 (activist target). $1B+ cumulative investment at risk.
5. **Legal ceiling on IP enforcement.** 9th Circuit revived CREXi antitrust counterclaims in June 2025 — enforcement posture has limits.

## Catalyst Path
1. **Activist resolution** — Third Point/D.E. Shaw proxy outcome; Homes.com shutdown, sale, or accelerated profitability.
2. **Apartments.com standalone disclosure** — any management move to separately report Apartments.com economics.
3. **Commercial pricing stabilization** — evidence that realized pricing-through-renewal holds above 3%.
4. **Homes.com structural transaction** — sale, spin-off, or JV that crystallizes or eliminates the capital drag.
5. **Q3'26 new-homes information module launch** — CoStar Suite product using Homes.com homebuilder data.

## Key Monitoring
1. **CoStar Suite gross renewal rate** — warning below 90% for 2 consecutive quarters.
2. **Realized pricing-through-renewal** — primary near-term kill: below 3% for 2 quarters.
3. **Apartments.com PM monthly renewal** — any decline from 99% is significant; below 95% = kill.
4. **Apartments.com brand awareness gap** — currently 31 pts (67% vs. 36%); warning below 20 pts.
5. **AI-native CRE data competitor traction** — any $100M+ funding round + Tier-1 institutional client.
6. **Homes.com agent subscriber growth** — below 30% YoY for 2 quarters + profitability timeline extension.
7. **Court rulings** — 9th Circuit antitrust counterclaim outcome.
