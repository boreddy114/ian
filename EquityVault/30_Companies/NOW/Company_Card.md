## One-Liner
ServiceNow owns an irreplaceable operational ontology — the CMDB/CSDM — encoding 20+ years of enterprise business logic, making it the scarce input AI agents need to act in the enterprise. The business is better than the market understands, but SBC drag, unresolved AI directional questions, and a Q1 print revealing organic guide trims and zero AI Control Tower customer proof points prevent a Buy rating at current prices.

**FY2025:** Revenue $13.3B (+20.9%), Sub Rev $12.9B (+21% CC), cRPO $12.85B (+25%), Non-GAAP OPM 31%, FCF $4.6B (35% margin), 98% renewal.

**Q1 2026:** Sub Rev 19% CC (in-line), cRPO 21% CC reported / ~20% organic (Moveworks +100bps), OPM 32% (beat), FCF 44.2%. FY26 guide raised to $15.755B sub rev (+20.5-21% CC), OPM cut 32%→31.5% (Armis), FCF cut 36%→35%.

## Bull Case
1. **Ontology moat validated as Fortress-class.** Pressure test upgraded placement from Contested to Fortress (Classical 3.8, Agentic Vuln 2.3). CMDB is verification-grade K_IP with 7–10 year rebuild reality; compliance and coordination are ~52% of switching costs (Category B).
2. **Now Assist consumption flywheel working.** Target raised to $1.5B FY26. Moveworks closed seven-figure deals in Q1 alone. Customers >$1M AI spend grew 130%+ YoY. Not shelf-ware.
3. **Security TAM expansion.** Armis ($7.75B) extends ontology into physical world. See (Armis) → Decide (Veza) → Act (ServiceNow). Security workflows +100% YoY in Q4'25. $125B addressable market.
4. **Partnership moat.** Microsoft, OpenAI, Anthropic partnering (not competing). NOW positioned to govern external AI agents entering the enterprise.
5. **Non-IT diversification complete.** >50% of incremental NNACV from non-IT workflows.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **SBC remains structurally elevated.** ~44% of revenue. Cash-adjusted FCF yield approaches zero. $5B buyback is dilution offset, not capital return. FY26 OPM and FCF guides both cut post-Armis.
2. **AI Control Tower has zero marquee customer proof points.** Bill pitched orchestration vision on Q1 call but couldn't name a single cross-platform customer. IBM architect calls it "not particularly differentiated." Block's open-source Goose (~29K GitHub stars) and Palantir's Foundry offer free/competing alternatives.
3. **Q2 organic cRPO guide implies ~275bps decel.** Buyside expected 18-19% organic; guide strips to 16-17.3%. This is the data point driving the post-Q1 sell-off.
4. **"Who Gets Paid?" question is structural.** When Copilot calls ServiceNow to execute a workflow, Microsoft may capture the economics. Risk of commoditization into middleware.
5. **Hyperscaler "free implementation" model.** Microsoft subsidizes Copilot adoption; NOW charges 20-25% more at renewal. Asymmetric competition in non-ITSM modules.
6. **Mid-market vulnerable.** AI-native ITSM startups (Serval, Console, Ravenna) deploying in days, winning quick-win execution layer.

## Catalyst Path
1. **Financial Analyst Day — May 4, 2026.** Either proves AI Control Tower differentiation with marquee customer + credible long-range FCF trajectory, or the post-Q1 move is the start of a re-rating, not the bottom.
2. **Q2 organic cRPO actual.** If prints 18-19% organic (vs. 16-17% guide), decel was timing. If at/below guide, trajectory is real.
3. **2H OPM ramp.** Must go from 26.5% Q2 to 33-34% 2H. Any miss compounds FCF problem.
4. **Armis integration.** Cross-sell traction within 12 months of close.
5. **SBC trajectory.** Two consecutive quarters declining as % of revenue would clear key upgrade condition.
