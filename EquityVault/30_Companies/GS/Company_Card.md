## One-Liner
Second-best quarter in firm history ($17.55 EPS, 19.8% ROE) driven by Equities financing (+59% y/y) and IB fees (+48%), with record $5B buyback; but FICC declined 10%, CET1 fell 180bps to 12.5% on $85B RWA growth, and the tax rate was flattered by $895M of share-based award benefits ($2.91 EPS impact) — core earnings power is strong but headline flatters the run-rate.

## Bull Case
1. **Dominant IB franchise** — IB fees +48% y/y to $2.84B; Advisory +10% q/q on completed M&A; ECM significantly higher on convertible offerings. #1 M&A franchise globally.
2. **Equities powerhouse** — $5.33B revenue (+27% y/y); financing +59% driven by prime brokerage; intermediation +7% on cash equities. Structural market share gains.
3. **Record buyback capacity** — $5.0B repurchased in Q1-26 (record), up from $3.0B in Q4-25. Average diluted shares -1.6%.
4. **AWM scaling toward 30% margin** — AUS at $3.65T with 33rd consecutive quarter of long-term net inflows ($62B); Industry Ventures acquisition adds $7B AUS.
5. **Management targets achievable** — 14-16% ROE (printed 19.8%), 15-17% ROTCE (printed 21.3%), 60% efficiency (printed 60.5%). Exceeding on all three.
6. **Constructive IB pipeline** — backlog decreased only slightly; sponsor activity is potential upside catalyst; Solomon sees "pretty constructive investment banking ecosystem."

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **FICC declined 10% y/y** — lower rates, mortgages, and credit products; intermediation -13%. FICC can be volatile and difficult to sustain.
2. **Tax rate flattery** — 13.2% effective rate vs. 21.4% full-year 2025; share-based award tax benefits of $895M added $2.91 to EPS and 310bps to ROE. Normalize to ~20% ETR and EPS is closer to $15.
3. **CET1 ratio fell 180bps to 12.5%** — RWAs rose $85B (credit + market); SLR dropped 60bps to 4.6%. Capital ratios declining as balance sheet expands to support client activity.
4. **AWM pre-tax margin at 23% vs. 30% target** — gap remains meaningful; lower deposit spreads on Marcus; investments revenue volatile.
5. **Sponsor activity still depressed** — private equity monetization remains slow; Solomon acknowledges sponsors have "been slower" with optionality.
6. **VaR jumped 40%** — average daily VaR rose from $80M to $112M, with rates and commodities VaR up significantly. Higher risk profile.
7. **Platform Solutions still messy** — Apple Card wind-down creates noise; segment economics remain unclear.

## Key Monitoring
1. **IB fee backlog trajectory** — decreased slightly in Q1; watch advisory vs. DCM/ECM mix. Sponsor activity unlocking would be a significant catalyst.
2. **FICC stabilization** — can GS arrest the decline in rates and mortgages? Commodities and FX strength needs to persist.
3. **CET1 capital trajectory** — 12.5% with 11.4% requirement; 110bps buffer is thinner than historical. RWA management discipline matters.
4. **AWM margin expansion** — path from 23% to 30% pre-tax margin is the key structural story. Industry Ventures integration and fee-based inflows are the drivers.
5. **Buyback sustainability** — $5B is a record; can they maintain $15-20B annualized? Capital generation vs. RWA growth is the constraint.
6. **Macro environment** — Solomon flagged volatility increasing, tariff concerns weighing on sentiment as Q1 progressed. If deal activity stalls, the IB beat is one-time.
