## Bull Case
1. **Tier 6 Coordination Equilibrium** — POA-based proxy chain cannot be forked; 30+ years of regulatory path dependence; NYSE rules explicitly reference BR as structural counterparty.
2. **Tokenization is a tailwind** — Probability-weighted scenarios (65-75% base + 10-25% bull) are neutral to positive; DLR platform tokenized $384B/day; Canton Network super-validator role.
3. **Underappreciated moat duration** — Market prices ~10-15 year moat duration; framework supports effectively permanent absent regulatory restructuring.
4. **98% revenue retention** — ~57% Category B (durable) switching costs in GTO; compliance/counterparty coordination dominates.
5. **Voting Choice expansion** — $4T AUM (up from $2T prior year); governance value-add expanding the revenue per position.
6. **Re-rating optionality** — Reclassification from SaaS to regulated infrastructure utility (V/MA/ICE comps) could deliver 30-50% multiple expansion.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Single-keystone risk** — All three moats (coordination equilibrium, SoR, network) depend on the SEC/NYSE/DTC regulatory framework; if it breaks, the stack unravels.
2. **Tokenization catastrophic scenario** — 10-15% probability of direct issuer-to-shareholder disintermediation; stock dropped 6.25% on Q2 FY26 print despite beat-and-raise on this fear.
3. **Legacy platform perception** — Grouped with "legacy software providers" in some expert assessments; modernization/AI deficit concerns raised by investors.
4. **Pricing power questions** — Regulated fee schedule caps upside; inflation-linked increases may mask underlying competitive vulnerabilities.
5. **Closed sales softness** — $288M in FY25 vs. $342M prior year (-16%); concern if sustained below $250M.

## Key Monitoring
1. Equity revenue position growth (currently 11% Q2 FY26; healthy if ≥5-7%).
2. Revenue retention rate (currently 98%; concern threshold <95%).
3. SEC concept releases or proposed rules on direct issuer-to-shareholder communication.
4. DTCC tokenized proxy chain announcements (integration vs. replacement of BR).
5. Tokenization revenue scaling (currently $7M Q2 FY26; target >$25M annual by FY28).
6. Voting Choice adoption ($4T AUM; leading indicator of governance value-add expansion).
