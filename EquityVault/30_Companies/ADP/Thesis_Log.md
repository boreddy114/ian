**Source:** Catalini, Hui & Wu (2026) Agentic Vulnerability Framework applied to ADP

### Composite Scores

| Dimension | Score |
|---|---|
| Classical Moat | 4.2 / 5 |
| Agentic Vulnerability | 2.0 / 5 |
| **Classification** | **FORTRESS** |

### Key Insight

ADP's switching costs are structurally bifurcated in its favor. ~55–60% of total switching cost burden falls into Category B (coordination and compliance = durable, non-automatable), significantly above the enterprise SaaS median (~30–40%). The payroll engine (Category A) is replicable; the regulatory infrastructure, fiduciary custody, tax authority coordination, PEO co-employment, and multi-party coordination (Category B) are not.

The agentic economy deepens this moat rather than eroding it — as AI automates routine HCM tasks, the verification and compliance layer becomes the irreducible human bottleneck.

## 2026-03 — CEO/CFO Meeting Debrief: Thesis Framework Established

**Source:** Meeting with Maria Black (CEO) + Peter Hadley (CFO), March 2026
**Price at time:** ~$255 (pre-selloff)

### Thesis Reframing

The market's problem: ADP trades down 23% from highs. Jefferies downgraded to Underperform ($230 PT) arguing (1) $1B+ payroll providers tripled from 2 to 6, (2) medium-term targets aspirational as PPC flattens and float fades, (3) AI-driven employment reduction is structural threat, (4) valuation full.

Our reframing: ADP is not a pure software company. The payroll calculation engine is the least important part. The company is regulated financial infrastructure:

- **Layer 1 (Payroll Engine):** NO MOAT — rules-based, codifiable. Gusto, Rippling, Deel all built modern engines.
- **Layer 2 (Financial Infrastructure):** STRONG MOAT — $3.1T annual money movement, 78M W-2s, money transmission licenses in 50 states, banking relationships.
- **Layer 3 (Fiduciary/Risk Transfer):** STRONGEST MOAT — 190K 401(k) plans, PEO co-employer for 158K WSEs, fiduciary duty. An AI agent cannot be a co-employer or take fiduciary responsibility.
- **Layer 4 (Distribution/Trust):** REAL BUT ERODING — 67K employees, 1.1M clients, 75+ year brand. Generational risk: younger founders may never consider ADP.

### Key Meeting Takeaways

**Positive signals:**
- Not seeing AI-driven displacement in PPC or WSE data
- Not seeing lower attach rates or trade-down (contrasts with PAYX)
- PEO is NOT concentrated in white-collar professional services (pushed back hard on Jefferies)
- PEO is "furthest thing from pure software" — co-employment, risk transfer, fiduciary duty
- Tracking toward mid-to-upper end of 4–7% bookings range

**Yellow flags:**
- Could not quantify why 27M records produce better AI than 7M — the data moat is either unquantified or smaller than assumed
- Clover embedded payroll channel is "early and not yet material"
- Float advantage is cyclical, not permanently structural
- Headcount cuts will come through attrition/automation, not headline RIF — limits near-term EPS upside catalysts

### Insurance Broker Analogy

Marsh, Aon, Willis, AJG, Brown & Brown face similar AI disruption fears as people-based middlemen. But to bind insurance, you need a legal entity — an agent can't bind insurance because there's a liability transfer requiring a legal counterparty. ADP's fiduciary and co-employment roles serve the same function. The calculation can be automated; the legal liability cannot.

### Bottom Line at Time of Meeting

More constructive than Jefferies downgrade thesis. Moats deeper than market appreciates because of misclassification. But stock needs a catalyst (bookings acceleration, macro improvement, or AI monetization clarity) to rerate from ~24x NTM P/E.

*Note: Stock subsequently sold off to ~$210 (~17.8x NTM) by late April, making the valuation case considerably stronger while the thesis remains unchanged.*
