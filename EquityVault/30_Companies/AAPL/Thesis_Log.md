## April 22, 2025 — Worrisome Signs in the App Store

**Source:** Apple Yes, but… Worrisome Signs in the App Store...pdf (MoffettNathanson, Craig Moffett)

### Rating: Neutral | PT: $270

### Key Findings

**US App Store growth is bifurcating dangerously**
- US App Store revenue grew just +0.1% y/y in calendar Q1-26
- Ex-US grew +11.1% — the ~11pp US-vs-ex-US gap is yawning
- Gap concentrated in subscription-heavy categories: Books (-43%), Lifestyle (-34%), Entertainment (flat-to-negative)
- These are exactly the businesses with incentive to flip to web billing after May 2025 Epic contempt ruling

**AI is the only US growth driver**
- US Productivity revenue +70% y/y (+$214M) driven by ChatGPT and AI subscriptions
- Ex-Productivity, US App Store revenue is down ~3% y/y
- When AI subscriptions mature and developers optimize for web billing, the gap widens

**Netflix precedent is instructive**
- When Netflix pulled iOS signups in late 2018, iOS revenue went from 2.9% of Netflix total (2018) to 0.2% (2025)
- iOS MAU more than doubled during that period — users stayed, revenue didn't
- Now it's easier than ever for developers to funnel iOS users to web checkouts

**Services may be strong enough to absorb it — for now**
- Google TAC, Search Ads, iCloud, etc. may brush off App Store deceleration
- But App Store is the cornerstone of the Services moat
- MoffettNathanson maintains Neutral at $270 on 28x FY27 EPS with 8% WACC, 4.6% terminal growth

## November 18, 2025 — Gatekeeping: Edge AI Compute Platform Thesis

**Source:** Apple Gatekeeping Apple Gatekeeping.pdf (Arete Research, Richard Kramer)

### Rating: Buy | PT: $305 (from $277)

### Key Thesis Points

**Apple is the gatekeeper of edge AI compute**
- 1.3B iOS installed base with tight control over silicon, OS, and end-user utilities
- Billions in consumer hardware losses at GOOG, META, MSFT, AMZN, OAI implicitly validate Apple's moat
- Apple has years to leverage edge compute position (highly capable silicon in iPhones) for AI-powered services

**Eight underappreciated factors for FY26-27**
1. Channel inventory rebuild can boost sales
2. New products in FY27: smartglasses (iWear) and foldables (iPhone Air Max)
3. Mac market share gains opportunity
4. China smartphone share rebound
5. In-house silicon efforts driving $5B+ COGS savings (C1X, N1 chips)
6. Services slowing inevitable — AppStore fee concessions likely — but offset by AppleOne price hikes
7. Apple entering investment cycle, more cash to R&D
8. Apple-Google "Grand Bargain" — revenue share evolving from Search Ads to Gemini integration

## October 7, 2025 — Harvest Moon: iPhone 17 Super-Cycle Setup

**Source:** Apple Harvest Moon Apple Harvest Moon.pdf (Arete Research, Richard Kramer)

### Rating: Buy | PT: $277 (from $250)

### Key Points
- iPhone 17 new form factors (Air replaced Plus at $100 higher price, bumped Pro prices) driving "super-cycle"
- Apple defended installed base in FY25 with trade-in deals; now going on offense as rivals struggle
- Set to exceed $1T of iPhone sales in last 5 years with 1.3B iOS installed base
- Android vendors mostly taking share from each other, not eating into Apple's base
- AI not yet a driver of phone purchases — Apple supporting margins with higher prices and in-house silicon
- Siri-Gemini deal could shift perception from "lacking innovation" to "secure private-edge compute + new form factors"
- Applying 30x FY2 P/E to FY26E EPS of $9 + $2/share cash, blended with DCF = $277 PT
