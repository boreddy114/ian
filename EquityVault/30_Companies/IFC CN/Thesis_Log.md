### Where the Beat Came From
Of the $0.27 beat vs. consensus, the bulk is investment income — operating NII +10% y/y to $457M, with management citing special dividends and raising 2026 NII guidance to ~$1.7B from $1.6B. Distribution income was actually down 2% y/y at $115M, against ≥10% annual growth target. Non-operating tax line also helped. Underwriting income only grew 4% to $504M.

### Key Findings

**The headline 91.3% C/R is not what it looks like**
- Underlying current-year loss ratio deteriorated 1.2pts to 61.5% from 60.3%
- Offset by 2.0pts of favorable cat variance (2.4% vs. 4.4% LY) and flat PYD at 7.1%
- PYD of 7.1% vs. management's 2-4% normalized guidance — strip PYD to 4% and group C/R is ~94.4%
- Strip to midpoint (3%) and you're at 96%
- Smoothed 4Q25 view of 92.5% looks closer to right than the print suggests

**Segment dispersion confirms the cycle is bifurcating**

**Cdn Personal Auto — the clean story:** 9% premium growth on 1% unit growth = 8pts of pure pricing. Underlying LR improved 2.2pts. Textbook hard market. Whether Ontario July reforms or Alberta Jan 2027 reforms break the cycle is a 2027 question.

**Cdn Commercial — the canary:** DPW growth of 2% down from 5%+ trajectory. Headline 86.2% C/R held by 12.3% favorable PYD on long-tail lines. Underlying CY LR worsened 4.5pts to 57.4% with 5pts of higher large losses. Without PYD, this segment runs in the high 90s.

**UK&I went the wrong way:** 103.2% C/R, +5.6pts y/y. Underlying CY LR rose 2.8pts to 61.6% with 4pts of higher large losses. Expense ratio +2pts y/y. Management claims segment is "well positioned to evolve toward 90%" — same language as February when 4Q25 printed 93.5%. Going backwards 10pts in one quarter stretches credibility. UK&I is 24% of group premium.

**US — unsung hero:** 83.4% C/R, 4% growth in constant currency on sub-90% lines.

**PYD is doing more work than investors will pay for**
- Reserve releases are real cash, but the multiple assigned to them is far below current-year underwriting margin
- Market's read: one-time, draws down a future asset, tells nothing about underlying trajectory
- At 92.5% smoothed C/R, EPS would be ~$14.91 vs. reported $19.21, putting the multiple at ~17x rather than ~13x
- Market has been right to keep the multiple at this level

**Capital story genuinely improved**
- $4.0B capital margin = $1.5B above "normal operating level"
- Adjusted debt-to-cap dropped to 16.4% from 19.1%, well below 20% target
- NCIB renewed for up to 3% of float; 595k shares bought in Q1 ($154M), additional 247.5k ($63M) in early Q2
- ~$650M annualized buyback pace + M&A firepower
- Brindamour: capital generation "allows us to deploy capital on share buybacks when our shares are meaningfully undervalued"
- This is a floor on the multiple, not a re-rating catalyst

### Rating & Action
- **Rating:** Neutral (downgraded from Hold). Continue trim toward zero.
- **Upgrade to Buy conditions:** (1) UK&I prints below 95% in Q2 with underlying LR normalizing toward 60%; (2) Cdn Commercial reaccelerates above 5% DPW growth with stable underlying LR; (3) management walks down PYD assumption and underlying C/R still compresses.
- **Downgrade to Avoid conditions:** (1) Two consecutive quarters of underlying LR deterioration; (2) UK&I 12-month glide path explicitly walked back; (3) PYD reverts to 2-4% guide and headline C/R moves above 95%.
- **Next decision point:** Q2-26 results, late July. UK&I print is the swing variable.
