## One-Liner
Spotify is the undisputed streaming audio leader at 293M premium subs, transitioning from a volume-driven growth story to a pricing + AI personalization + multi-format engagement story — but gross margin expansion is at risk as all three major label groups have renewed deals with wholesale rate escalators that the Street hasn't fully priced.

## Bull Case
1. **AI personalization compounding engagement:** DJ at 94M users (near 100M), SongDNA at 52M users in 4 weeks, Prompted Playlists expanding. "Large Personalization Model" trained on proprietary taste data no competitor can replicate.
2. **Pricing power demonstrated:** US price hike (Jan '26) showed no unusual churn — second round even less impactful than first. BLS data suggests 60% of sub base from $100K+ households with low elasticity.
3. **Multi-format monetization unlocked:** Free → Premium → Add-on (audiobooks) captures the full power law of engagement. Fitness hub with Peloton as latest vertical.
4. **Productivity gains from AI:** Shipping velocity doubled (DODs, pull requests); headcount flat while output 2x+. Compute per employee up, not headcount.
5. **761M MAU + 293M subs = platform scale:** Global, platform-agnostic, 20-year data moat on listening behavior and taste.
6. **Investor Day May 21:** Catalyst for re-rating if management delivers on near/medium-term initiative detail.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Gross margin expansion at risk from label deals:** All three Majors renewed in 2024-2025 with wholesale rate escalators. MoffettNathanson: "monotonic increases in expected gross margins imply labels continue to give up leverage — we're skeptical." Street models +130bps GM expansion in '26; MN more cautious.
2. **Ad business struggling to scale:** Digital audio is ~$8B in US vs. $260B+ total digital ads. Video pivot needed but risks alienating core music audience. Ad revenue +3% FXN in Q1 — well below digital ad market growth.
3. **2Q guide disappointed:** Gross margin 33.1% (vs. 33.3% consensus), op income €630M (vs. €678M consensus). "Increased investments" without specifics until Investor Day.
4. **Labels retain leverage:** Catalog ownership is the ultimate moat — 80/0.2 power law (80% of streams from 0.2% of tracks). Music catalogs are homogeneous across SAPs (no exclusivity).
5. **Apple and YouTube can subsidize music:** Apple One bundles music at marginal cost; YouTube Premium bundles ad-free video + music. Neither needs standalone music economics.
6. **Valuation stretched if GM stalls:** At ~11x '27 EV/Gross Profit (RJ PT), any GM disappointment compresses multiples quickly. Stock -45% from 52-week high.

## Key Monitoring
1. Q2'26 gross margin (guided 33.1%) — any upside surprise signals label deal impact less severe.
2. Investor Day (May 21) — must deliver specifics on AI products, fitness vertical, and margin path.
3. Ad-supported revenue acceleration in H2'26 — management committed to improved growth.
4. Premium ARPU trajectory — pricing actions flowing through vs. mix dilution from emerging markets.
5. Label deal economic impact — watch incremental royalty cost growth vs. premium revenue growth (MN's key chart).
6. Video/social pivot execution — can SPOT capture video ad dollars without alienating music-first users?
