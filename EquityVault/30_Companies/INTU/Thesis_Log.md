### Revised Buffett Answer
"In five years, Intuit's moat will be roughly the same — not wider, not dramatically narrower. The QuickBooks general ledger remains the system of record for millions of SMBs and the compliance/coordination switching costs are durable. But the moat is being monetized less effectively (point solution cannibalization, pricing backlash from accountants), the mid-market expansion is defensive retention rather than offensive conquest, the consumer data flywheel leaked when Mint was shuttered, and the AI flywheel is decelerating under privacy friction. The company will be fine. The stock may still be cheap. But the thesis is 'resilient incumbent at a discount' not 'compounding fortress.'"

### Position Sizing Revised

- **Revised: small-to-medium position**
- CAP gap narrows from 4–6 years to 1–3 years. Still a mispricing, but lower conviction and narrower margin of safety.

## March 2026 — Research Handoff & Diligence Log

**Source:** INTU Research Handoff March2026.docx
**Rating:** NEUTRAL — Under Active Research

### Research Arc
INTU entered the research universe as a new-to-us name — stock down ~32% over 6 months against IGV down 13%. Central question: is the market right to structurally de-rate INTU because AI commoditizes its products, or is the market wrong?

### AGI Framework Application (Catalini/Hui/Wu, Feb 2026)
Applied the four-zone model to INTU:
- **Safe Industrial Zone** (low cA, low cH): TurboTax DIY, routine QB bookkeeping → commoditization risk
- **Runaway Risk Zone** (low cA, high cH): Complex tax planning → dangerous deployment zone
- **Human Artisan Zone** (high cA, low cH): Audit representation → human labor retains value
- **Pure Tacit Zone**: Brand trust, relationship capital → coordination economics

**Key Insight — The Codifier's Curse:** Every TurboTax Live interaction trains Intuit Assist. ~25% of TurboTax users use the AI copilot. Intuit is simultaneously building the AI product that makes TT Live less necessary while charging the Live premium that funds it.

### TurboTax Complexity Distribution (IRS SOI TY2022, 161.3M returns)

| Tier | Returns | % of Total | TT Live Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 3 — Genuinely Complex | 8–9M | 5–6% | With CPAs already |
| Tier 2 — Anxious Middle | 20–25M | ~15% | **TT Live's real market** |
| Tier 2b — Borderline Simple | 15–20M | ~10% | Highly vulnerable to AI |
| Tier 1 — Simple | 112–118M | 70–73% | Free tier / embedded fintech |

### Central Analytical Question: Confidence vs. Liability Transfer
When a TT Live customer pays $150–$400, are they paying for:
- **(a) Liability transfer** — CPA signs, absorbs regulatory risk, can be subpoenaed. Structurally durable.
- **(b) Psychological confidence** — the feeling it's correct. Vulnerable to AI with credible guarantees.

Expert calls confirmed upgrade behavior is primarily **anxiety-driven** — life events reset confidence even when complexity doesn't increase.

### Vertical Threat Analysis: QB Moat Confirmed
11 platforms studied. Universal finding: every vertical SaaS platform has chosen to be accounting-agnostic. ServiceTitan's Touchless Integration actually **reinforces** QB by eliminating batch export friction. Only displacement: AppFolio in property management (trust accounting is genuinely vertical-specific — exception, not template).

## March 2026 — AI Tax Startup Pitch Deck & Data Project

**Source:** INTU DataProject TaxLiability March2026.docx
**Rating:** N/A (research methodology)

### Falsifiable Hypothesis
**Null (what Intuit claims):** Professional preparation produces materially better audit outcomes. The $150–$400 premium reflects genuine liability reduction.

**Alternative (what we need to prove):** On a complexity-controlled basis, professionally prepared returns do NOT produce better audit outcomes. The Live premium is primarily anxiety-driven.

### Key Actuarial Data Points
- Overall IRS audit rate: 0.19% (FY2024)
- Core TT demographic ($50K–$200K): ~0.10% (1 in 1,000)
- GAO NRP analysis: paid preparer returns had **60% error rate** vs. 50% for self-prepared
- Expected loss per insured simple return: ~$0.69
- At a $29 price point, loss ratio is ~5–8% before expenses → extremely favorable for insurance underwriting

### The AI Entrant's Marketing Pitch (Data-Validated)
"Professional tax preparation costs $300 and produces errors 60% of the time. Our AI costs $0. We'll insure your return for $20." The psychological premium Intuit charges is not supported by outcome data at the population level.

### Critical Data Gap
What the public data CANNOT tell us: how do TurboTax Live Full Service customers perform at audit vs. DIY customers of equivalent complexity? Intuit has this data. Almost certainly doesn't publish it. This is the single most important data point in the thesis.
