## One-Liner
META printed its strongest revenue growth in 5 years (+33%) but the dominant story is the magnitude of the AI spending commitment — $135B capex midpoint, $107B incremental contractual obligations, 1GW custom silicon — all funding Zuckerberg's distribution-led personal-agent strategy that is structurally different from lab-first competition.

## Bull Case
1. **Ad system LLM-scale models now in production:** Trillion-parameter adaptive ranking model, +6% conversion lift on landing page ads, value optimization ARR >$20B (doubled YoY).
2. **Distribution moat for AI agents:** 3.35B+ daily users across Family of Apps — Zuckerberg's lane is "billions" via distribution vs. competitors serving "hundreds of thousands."
3. **Recommendation engine driving real engagement:** +10% lift in time spent on Facebook from Q1 ranking improvements; +9% FB video watch time in US/Canada.
4. **Custom silicon at scale:** 1GW+ with Broadcom plus significant AMD chips — joining MSFT/AMZN/GOOG in proprietary inference cost advantage.
5. **Muse Spark / MSL:** First Meta Superintelligence Labs model shipped in ~10 months from lab standup — double-digit % increases in Meta AI sessions per user.
6. **May RIF + flat-to-down headcount:** "Leaner operating model" offsets investment spend structurally.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Capex magnitude is staggering:** FY26 $125-145B (raised from $115-135B); $107B incremental contractual commitments in Q1 alone; "continued to underestimate compute needs" for FY27.
2. **No quantitative ROI signposts on AI investment:** Zuckerberg explicitly refused milestones — framework is quality → scale → monetization → efficiency. Market must take this on faith.
3. **Agentic monetization undefined:** Both personal agents (commissions/premium) and business AI (paid model) deferred to future — "this year is key for establishing agents as the vehicle."
4. **Third-party cloud deals signal capacity constraints:** First acknowledgment META is using third-party cloud at meaningful scale for 2027 — adding opex on top of capex.
5. **Regulatory and antitrust overhang:** FTC case, EU DMA, global AI regulation all create tail risk for the advertising monopoly.
6. **Stock reaction to spending signals market patience wearing thin:** -7% on a +33% revenue print says the market is focused on the denominator (capex), not the numerator (revenue).
