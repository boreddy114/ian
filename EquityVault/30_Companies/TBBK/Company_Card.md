## One-Liner
TBBK is a BaaS platform bank earning 35% ROE by providing the regulated banking infrastructure (cards, deposits, lending) behind major fintechs like Chime and Cash App, with three growth initiatives — credit-sponsored lending, embedded finance, and new program onboarding — set to drive EPS from $5.90 ('26) to $8.00+ ('27).

## Business Mix

| Segment | Description | Key Metrics |
|---|---|---|
| Fintech Solutions (Prepaid/Debit) | Card issuance, GDV processing, interchange | GDV +18% YoY; 15 verticals including government |
| Credit-Sponsored Lending | Balance sheet lending with full credit enhancement from fintech partners | $1.65B (+50% Q/Q from $1.1B); 21% of loans, targeting 30-40% |
| Real Estate Bridge Lending (REBL) | Workforce housing repositioning loans | 31% of loans; criticized assets -29% Q/Q; yields declining (8.5% → 7.6%) |
| SBLOC/IBLOC | Securities-backed and insurance-backed lending | 23% of loans; deemphasizing to make room for fintech |
| SBA Lending | Small business loans | 14% of loans |
| Off-Balance Sheet Deposits | Excess deposits swept to third-party banks | $1.34B (+58% Q/Q); $900K fee income in Q1 |

**Q1'26:** EPS $1.41 (+18% YoY), ROE 35.1%, ROA 2.57%. NIM 3.87% (-43bps Q/Q on fintech mix shift). Revenue growth +15% YoY.

## Bull Case
1. **Credit-sponsored lending is a flywheel:** $1.65B (+50% Q/Q), fully credit-enhanced (zero loss risk to TBBK), targeting 30-40% of balance sheet over 3-4 years. Two new partners expected in '26.
2. **Chime relationship economics are a "3% NIM short-term bond" with zero credit risk** + interchange + deposit funding + GDV spend — unique, deep, multi-product relationship.
3. **Cash App launch ramping:** Passed first gate, now in "turn the dial" stage. Zero contribution in Q1; meaningful by Q4'26, inflection in early '27.
4. **EPS trajectory is steep:** $5.90 ('26E) → $8.00+ ('27E). Q4'26 target $1.75 implies ~$7.00 run-rate. APEX 2030 targets: 4% ROA, 40% ROTCE.
5. **Balance sheet velocity accelerating:** New partners will use securitization (3-30 day hold), not balance-sheet-heavy Chime model — fee income becomes bigger lever.
6. **Capital return aggressive:** $200M buybacks in '26 ($50M/quarter); 100% of net income in '27. Equity stays flat → ROE/ROA compound.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **EPS ramp is extremely back-half loaded:** $1.41 Q1 → $1.75 Q4 target requires near-doubling of quarterly earnings. Dependent on partner timelines TBBK doesn't control.
2. **NIM compression structural as mix shifts:** NIM fell 43bps Q/Q to 3.87% as fintech lending (blended ~3% NIM) replaces traditional lending (~7% NIM). Fee income must offset.
3. **Partner concentration risk:** Chime is the dominant relationship. Cash App is the second. Two partners driving the thesis creates key-account dependency.
4. **Aubrey property still on books:** Operating breakeven by end of Q2'26, stabilization target Q1'27. Carrying cost risk extends ~12 months vs. prior mid-'26 exit framing.
5. **REBL yield compression:** Loans rolling off in low 8s, being put on in mid 6s. 1/3 of book is variable-rate tracking short rates down. Lower-risk but also lower-return portfolio going forward.
6. **Regulatory wildcard:** Proposed executive order requiring citizenship info for bank accounts could be "very difficult" for prepaid — core franchise asset.
