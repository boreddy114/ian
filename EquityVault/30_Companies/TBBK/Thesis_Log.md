## April 24, 2026 — Q1'26 Earnings: Credit Sponsorship Soars, Cash App Ramping

**Source:** TBBK_1Q26_Update_Off_the_Call_1.docx; 1Q26 Transcript.pdf

### Headline
EPS $1.41 (+18% YoY), ROE 35.1%, ROA 2.57%. Credit-sponsored lending balances surged to $1.65B (+50% non-annualized Q/Q). Guidance maintained: $5.90 FY26, $1.75 Q4'26, $8.10-8.30 FY27.

### Key New Information from the Call

**1. Strategic balance sheet allocation revised meaningfully higher:**
- Management willing to dedicate 30-40% of balance sheet to credit-sponsored loans over 3-4 years (up from original APEX 2030 thinking of 10%). Currently at 21%.
- Implies continued NIM compression optically, but offset by fee income + deposit cost reduction + securitization velocity.

**2. Fintech reserve coverage drop — clean answer:**
- Secured card product (cash-collateralized, fully credit-enhanced) outperformed in Q1 growth, shifting mix toward structurally lower-reserve product.
- Lower coverage ratio = safer underlying mix, NOT relaxed credit standards. **Upgrade from "watch item" to "understood and benign."**

**3. Chime blended NIM disclosed for first time:**
- ~3% blended NIM on entire Chime fintech lending relationship.
- "It's almost a bond, a 3% short-term bond that's yielding 3% and then you have all these other revenue streams coming off that."
- No credit risk (indemnified), fraction of origination cost of traditional lending, creates incremental interchange and GDV spend.

**4. Two new partners will be securitization-led:**
- New credit-sponsorship partners will hold loans 3-30 days max, then securitize — "incredibly high velocity."
- Fee-income stream is bigger lever than balance sheet for new partners (contrasts with Chime's balance-sheet-heavy model).

**5. Cash App: zero Q1 contribution, on track for H2:**
- "Very little" contribution in Q1. Partner is "meticulous" — long testing phase, first gate passed.
- $5.90 full-year does NOT depend on Cash App ramping fast — depends on it ramping on schedule in H2.
- $1.65B fintech loan growth in Q1 was Chime alone running hot. Good signal for YE26 exit rate.

**6. Aubrey: longer timeline, higher exit price:**
- Operating breakeven by end of Q2'26. Stabilization now targeted Q1'27 (shifted from prior mid-'26).
- Appraisals low-50s. Occupancy target high 80s/low 90s. "Rounding error to our buyback" upon exit.
- Directionally positive — optimizing price over timing — but carrying cost extends ~12 months.

**7. REBL yield compression is structural:**
- 1/3 of book is variable-rate (tracks short rates down). Large 2022-vintage bubble now refinancing at lower rates with stronger sponsors.
- Forward run-rate: loans rolling off in low 8s, put on in mid 6s.
- Lower-yielding, lower-risk portfolio going forward. Adds to NIM pressure.

**8. Off-balance sheet sweep is small:**
- Kozlowski: "gravy" — "not a big part of our own planning." Q1 seasonally high from tax season.
- $900K revenue is probably close to quarterly run-rate, not leading edge of bigger number.

**9. New regulatory wildcard:**
- Proposed executive order requiring banks to obtain citizenship info. "Very difficult" to implement for prepaid.
- TBBK has "fairly good information" relative to peers but did not dismiss implementation risk.
- Worth flagging for watch list — prepaid is core franchise asset.

## January 30, 2026 — Q4'25 Earnings + FY26 Guidance Initiated

**Source:** 4Q25 Transcript.pdf; TBBK 4Q25 Follow-Up (Raymond James)

### Headline
EPS $1.28 (core $1.31, below $1.46 consensus). Stock fell ~16%. FY26 guidance of $5.90 missed $6.43 consensus. BUT: Q4'26 target of $1.75 maintained; FY27 preliminary guidance $8.25.

### Key Developments
- **Credit sponsorship ended at $1.1B** (+40% Q/Q, +142% YoY) — exceeded $1B goal
- **Cash App partnership on track for early '26 launch**
- **Embedded finance platform development on pace** — launch expected early '26
- **Criticized assets -28% Q/Q** to $194M; delinquency declined from 2.19% to 1.60%
- **$150M share repurchases in Q4** ($375M for full year = 12% of shares)
- **Record ROE of 30.4% in Q4, 28.9% for FY25**

### RJ View (Strong Buy, $76 PT)
- "The Flywheel Isn't Broken, It Just Spins Later"
- EPS shortfall driven by: government shutdown impact on payments/deposits, credit sponsorship growth back-end loaded in Q4, legal settlement
- Reiterated $76 PT based on 10.8x run-rate Q4'26E EPS of $7.00
- "Earnings power and cash generation of TBBK's BaaS platform cannot be overlooked"
- 5-year EPS CAGR of 28% implied

### Balance Sheet Evolution
- Management signaled willingness to allocate 30-40% of balance sheet to credit sponsorship (up from 10% original plan)
- Will deemphasize IBLOC and RIA acquisition lending to make room
- SBA guaranteed paper and REBL loans can be distributed if needed
- "Real trick of our balance sheet is velocity" — set up to distribute loans through securitization/conduit structures
