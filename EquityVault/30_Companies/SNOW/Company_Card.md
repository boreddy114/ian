## One-Liner
Snowflake is a high-quality cloud data platform with real switching costs rooted in data gravity and pipeline integration, but ~58% of that switching cost is automatable migration labor, the wrapper agent disintermediation thesis is confirmed and already observable at major enterprises, and pricing power is actively eroding — making it a Contested moat that the market prices as if it were a Fortress.

## Business Mix

| Segment | Contribution | Growth | Key Metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| Analytics | Core revenue driver | ~30% YoY | NRR 125% |
| Data Engineering | Growing share | Strong | Competing with Databricks |
| AI (Cortex AI, Snowflake ML, Snowpark) | Emerging | >$100M run rate | 70% of customers view as cost-adding |
| Applications & Collaboration (Marketplace) | Early | Growing | 2,700+ listings, 670+ providers |

**FY2026:** Total revenue ~$4.7B (+29% YoY). Q4 product revenue $1.23B (+30%). NRR 125%. RPO $9.77B (+42%). 733 customers >$1M TTM. 766 Forbes G2000. Non-GAAP OPM 10.5% (FY27 guide: 12.5%). GAAP operating loss ~$1.5B (SBC ~$1.3B). Stock ~$136, market cap ~$47B (~10x EV/Rev).

## Bull Case
1. **AI consumption inflects.** Cortex AI revenue scales to $500M+ by FY2028. AI workloads create new lock-in layer for governance-heavy customers.
2. **Marketplace reaches critical mass.** Cross-customer data sharing becomes must-have. Secure Data Sharing and data clean rooms create network-level value.
3. **Governance advantage in regulated sectors.** IQVIA migrated toward Snowflake for healthcare compliance. MasterControl moved off Databricks for governance. Compliance anchor (Tier 4 sub-segment) provides durable floor.
4. **Databricks IPO forces pricing discipline.** Public-market scrutiny may moderate Databricks' aggressive 10-20% undercutting.
5. **RPO strength provides visibility.** $9.77B RPO (+42% YoY) with record $400M single Q4 deal. Customers committing more capital forward despite competitive pressure.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Pricing power actively eroding.** Deal sizes down 30-40%. Headroom purchases down 30% YoY. Multi-year deals down 20%. Snowflake offering 2-7% higher discounts YoY. Databricks undercutting 10-20% on compute.
2. **Wrapper agent disintermediation confirmed and observable.** Citigroup deployed Glean for cross-platform querying. Netflix uses semantic embeddings for unified agent layer. FanDuel built cross-platform workflows. dbt abstracts logic warehouse-agnostically. Zero market transcripts investigate this — widest analytical edge.
3. **AI features are cost-adding, not cost-saving.** 70% of one consultant's base views Cortex/Snowpark as incremental spend. Larger orgs prototype in Cortex then migrate mature logic to cheaper environments.
4. **Databricks competitive momentum.** $5.4B ARR, 65%+ growth, $134B private valuation. Winning ~70% of net-new AI/ML deals per SI partners. Partner economics shifting Databricks-first.
5. **GAAP profitability absent.** ~$1.3B annual SBC. Non-GAAP margin expanding but GAAP operating loss persists. Never generated GAAP operating income.
6. **Open format erosion.** Iceberg adoption structurally reduces proprietary format lock-in. Snowflake's own Iceberg support is a double-edged sword — more interoperable but more portable.

**Market Pricing Gap:** Overpriced Lock-In. At ~10x EV/Revenue, implied CAP is 5-8 years. Framework supports 3-5 years for a Tier 3 Contested moat. The 2-3 year gap is the overpricing. Widest edge: wrapper agent disintermediation is confirmed by practitioners but zero market transcripts investigated it.

## Catalyst Path
1. **Cortex AI revenue trajectory.** Must double annually. If AI revenue decelerates below 50% YoY, intelligence-layer thesis fails.
2. **NRR trajectory.** Holding 125% is critical. Drop below 120% for 2 quarters = active moat erosion signal.
3. **Databricks IPO.** Public-market scrutiny may change competitive dynamics and pricing behavior.
4. **RPO vs. revenue growth convergence.** RPO at 42% vs. revenue at 30% is positive. If RPO decelerates to revenue level, forward commitment weakening.
5. **Marketplace tipping point.** Cross-customer data sharing adoption.

## Key Monitoring
1. NRR — quarterly (kill condition: <115% for 2 consecutive quarters)
2. RPO growth vs. revenue growth gap
3. Iceberg adoption rate (data decoupling from compute)
4. AI revenue run rate and growth trajectory
5. Effective discount trends (2-7% YoY increases currently)
6. Wrapper agent adoption at enterprises (cross-platform querying becoming standard)
7. Marketplace listing growth and provider dual-listing on Databricks
8. Databricks SQL warehouse revenue growth post-IPO
