**Source:** Catalini moat re-test triggered by IGV bucketing exercise (durability × AI-coupling grid). Two Quartr transcripts + five AlphaSense Gen Search pulls (incl. one Databricks-targeted).

### Why re-opened
Bucketing software names by post-AI moat durability surfaced a hypothesis from Ian: SNOW may have been mis-rated because the *synthesis* of its moats (data + software + consumption + switching costs) could be stronger than the part-by-part screen credited. Ran it to ground.

### The reasoning arc (kept because the path matters for next time)
1. **Original screen:** CONTESTED. Classical 3.0 / Agentic 2.9. ~58% Category A (automatable migration labor) / 42% Category B (durable). "Customers choose it; they are not coordinated into it."

3. **Over-correction:** the Q3-governance and Q5 Gen Search answers (governance = "ultimate anchor," 70% Business Critical mix-shift) pulled me back too far toward "moat in transposition / firmer than screen." Caught by the no-capitulation discipline — I was reasoning off the *synthesis layer*, which systematically rounds toward stickiness.
4. **Raw transcript vs. synthesis check:** confirmed the Gen Search summaries laundered a single bullish SI's portfolio number into a "market fact" and folded the bearish insider into a "sticky anyway" narrative. The 70% figure is ONE consultant's ~$50M Southeast-US book, not audited market data.
5. **Outside-in pull + Databricks-targeted pull:** resolved it. The two genuinely non-aligned voices (ex-Databricks AE; Sun Life AVP who calls Databricks the leader) CONFIRMED the durable core against their own interest.

### Settled findings
- **Cortex / AI-execution moat: REFUTED.** Only 5-10% run mature ML in production on SNOW; serious ML exports to Databricks/SageMaker/Vertex; Databricks wins ~70% of advanced-ML bake-offs; agents run on external orchestration. The 70% Business Critical upsell is real but is a *governance/security* upsell, NOT AI-workload lock-in. Cortex drives renewals + BI retention, not stickiness.
- **Durable core CONFIRMED by adversarial sources, but narrow:** (a) regulated-vertical compliance/validation friction — migrated report outputs don't match legacy, triggers regulatory audits; liability-anchored, survives AI (AI tooling cuts effort 30-40%, can't eliminate redesign). (b) Clean-room / data-sharing coordination — Sun Life (Databricks-favoring) gave the mechanism: if host unplugs, the shared "play area" disappears, forcing partners to revert to SFTP/custom APIs. This is the counterparty-coordination answer that was unconfirmed across six prior pulls. Real Category B — but confined to the clean-room/sharing subset; report explicitly notes most enterprises use SNOW for internal analytics.
- **Vulnerable half CONFIRMED active:** cost is the live exit trigger ($2-5M compute = formal exit objective); Databricks undercuts 10-20% on compute, 30-50% TCO in migrations; documented exits (Fortune 100 retailer 400+TB/18mo/$3M; financial-services firm unwinding 200+ accounts) — kept quiet for negotiating leverage, which is WHY the SNOW-aligned sources under-reported them.
- **Threat is Databricks (ML/TCO) + Fabric (mid-market bundling), NOT Iceberg.** Every source across all seven pulls agrees the platform bifurcates by workload type.

### Net
Rating unchanged from original screen (CONTESTED, mid-band) — but every component now adversarially validated rather than ecosystem-asserted. The durable 42% is specifically regulated-vertical governance + clean-room coordination. The vulnerable 58% is ML + cost-sensitive analytics. NOT a melting ice cube; NOT a fortress. Bifurcates by workload.

### Methodological takeaway (the durable lesson)
AlphaSense Gen Search synthesis systematically rounds toward stickiness — it elevated one bullish SI's portfolio number to market fact and softened a bearish insider. Pulling raw transcripts and explicitly targeting adversarial (Databricks-side) sources was what corrected it. For future moat screens: do not rate off the synthesis layer; pull raw + adversarial before finalizing.
