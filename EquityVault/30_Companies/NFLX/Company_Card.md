## One-Liner
Netflix is the dominant premium narrative content aggregator, trading at a compressed ~28x 2027 EPS on a standalone ~20% EPS CAGR through 2030, with the WBD walk-away confirming capital discipline and the live/ads/pricing flywheel visibly intact. The thesis rests on digital scarcity of franchise IP in an AI-abundance world, non-extractive pricing providing a long monetization runway, and aggregator economics that make Netflix the platform rather than the content company the market still prices it as.

## Bull Case
1. **Non-extractive pricing runway.** Cost per viewing hour of ~$0.14 vs. competitors at $0.40-0.50. Two consecutive US price hikes in 15 months with retention improving across all four regions — the Marathon framework in its cleanest form.
2. **Ads flywheel at early innings.** Ads are only ~6% of revenue today; doubling to $3B in '26 with long-duration compounding from fill rates, programmatic mix, and CPM density over years.
3. **Digital scarcity in AI abundance.** Franchise IP (Stranger Things, Wednesday, Bridgerton, Squid Game, One Piece) has a 23% permanence rate vs. commodity content at 23% but with growing differentiation as AI floods the market with synthetic content.
4. **Live events solve churn.** "Can't stack and bank" content — at least one must-see event per month. WBC Japan was proof of concept; Tyson-Joshua UK and Concacaf Mexico are next tests.
5. **Capital discipline demonstrated.** Walked from WBD at $83B, collected $2.8B break fee, resumed buybacks ($1.3B Q1), deployed $600M into InterPositive (AI filmmaking). Management earned the discipline credit.
6. **Operating leverage.** Content spend falling from 42% → 32% of revenue by 2030 while growing in absolute terms drives OI margin from ~31.5% to ~41%.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **YouTube share gains.** YouTube has 13.4% of US TV time vs. Netflix at 8-9%, acquiring content for free. Structurally difficult to compete with infinite free content.
2. **Engagement plateau.** Total hours viewed grew ~1% YoY in H1 2025. Daily viewing per subscriber declining. US ~90% penetrated. Growth increasingly from lower-ARPU international markets.
3. **InterPositive patent-vs-positioning gap.** Publicly positioned as post-production tooling; granted patent describes a text-to-video model. Deployment scope creep is the risk — observable through credits, union filings.
4. **AI could eventually penetrate storytelling.** Peters' framework locates the bottleneck at scarce talent, not production — but a step-function AI improvement could move narrative storytelling from scarcity to abundance.
5. **Multiple permanently derated.** The WBD bid itself surfaced structural challenges; the old 35x multiple is probably not coming back regardless of deal outcome.
6. **Content quality drift.** Rotten Tomatoes scores on top-10 most-viewed originals declined ~10% between 2023-2025. HBO continues to dominate at the Emmys.

## Catalyst Path
1. **Q2'26 earnings (mid-July):** FY margin reaffirmation after content amort unwinds, ads pacing update, regional live rollout beyond Japan.
2. **Tyson-Joshua UK event:** Tests whether Japan WBC live playbook travels to a second region.
3. **Concacaf Mexico:** Third live region test.
4. **Harry Potter HBO series:** Even as a competitor production, fires the franchise re-ignition effect and informs library durability thesis.
5. **Ad revenue pacing:** Visibility on whether $3B '26 target becomes a floor rather than a ceiling.
6. **Buyback pacing:** $6.8B remaining on authorization.
