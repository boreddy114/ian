## One-Liner
Block's headline GP growth is 100% credit-led and halves when loan losses are properly burdened (~30% reported → ~15% adjusted), but the technology platform — the only closed-loop ecosystem owning merchant POS, consumer wallet, payment rails, and AI orchestration — is genuinely differentiated. The stock works on the AI narrative and accelerating EBIT; it breaks when the market re-describes it as a subprime lender. Neutral until Neighborhoods produces measurable results or credit-adjusted GP growth accelerates without balance sheet intensity.

## Bull Case
1. **Agentic commerce stack is real and proprietary.** Four-layer architecture (Moneybot → Weaver → Fillr → CDP → Draft Payment) built entirely in-house. No external LLM APIs. Capability surface grows automatically with each new product.
2. **Only true closed-loop ecosystem.** Block is the only company owning merchant POS + consumer wallet + payment rails + cross-merchant reward currency + AI orchestration. Toast has no consumer wallet. DoorDash has no POS. Stripe has no consumer app.
3. **Neighborhoods could un-invert the funnel.** At ~4.25% fully loaded merchant cost vs. DoorDash pickup commissions (6-15%), with consumer discovery + Local Cash rewards driving deposit-led relationships rather than credit-first.
4. **PBA conversion economics.** Primary Banking Actives generate ~10x GP of P2P-only users: $10.5-11K annual card spend, ~19 txns/month, 228 annual data points for underwriting.
5. **AI-driven cost transformation.** 40% RIF was real — enabled by Claude Code specifically. Goose orchestration across 26 revenue streams. Token cost in tens of millions, included in guidance.
6. **Stablecoin multi-chain readiness.** Cash App becoming a stablecoin on/off ramp (Solana, Ethereum, Arbitrum, Polygon). Potential non-credit revenue source if conversion fees materialize.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Credit-led growth is extractive and untested.** ~70% sub-585 FICO, 17.4 draws/year (perpetual recycler), 80% effective APR. Loan losses grew 359% YoY in 2025 in a benign macro environment.
2. **FCF is not what it appears.** Reported 2024 FCF was ~$2B; real FCF swung negative in 2025 as the loan book consumed cash. Street's $2.4B '26 estimate may not properly account for balance sheet intensity.
3. **Utah ILC charter is a regulatory end-run.** Federal preemption overrides state usury caps; CFPB is dormant but a future administration could revisit. Geographic reach contracts if challenged.
4. **Neighborhoods density problem.** Requires sufficient Square merchant × Cash App user overlap within a neighborhood. Q1 2026 is first clean quarter post-subsidy.
5. **Proprietary payment protocol vs. network effects.** Block building MRI/stablecoin rail while ACP (Walmart, Etsy, Instacart) and UCP (Shopify, Target, Wayfair) gain merchant adoption. Consumer-side infrastructure without merchant network.
6. **Narrative-reality gap.** Dorsey's shareholder letter description changes yearly while credit engine underneath does not. SBC is material; non-GAAP meaningfully overstates cash economics.

## Valuation
- **Forward P/E:** ~13.5x 2027 / ~10x 2028 GAAP-ish EPS (SBC expensed, severance excluded)
- **Context:** Not demanding for 20%+ EBIT growth, but if credit-adjusted GP growth is 15% not 30%, and balance sheet intensity consumes FCF, the multiple needs to compress or growth needs a non-credit source.
- **Downside scenario:** Market re-describes as subprime consumer lender → 8-10x earnings (specialty finance historical range) = ~40% downside on no change in fundamentals.
- **Upgrade triggers:** (1) Neighborhoods GPV disclosure + merchant retention post-subsidy, (2) credit-adjusted GP growth ≥20% without balance sheet intensity increase, (3) price correction to 8-10x forward.

## Catalyst Path
1. **Q1 2026 earnings:** First clean Neighborhoods data post-subsidy (Local Cash subsidy ended Jan 20). Merchant opt-out rates, order volume, GPV disclosure.
2. **Borrow delinquency data through any macro softening.** Untested cohort in downturn.
3. **Stablecoin public launch.** Fee structure, USDT support, multi-chain go-live timing.
4. **ETF product launch.** Commission structure, fractional ETF shares — investing TAM expansion.
5. **Moneybot GA rollout.** Currently gated behind Amplitude experiments; general availability would validate the agentic commerce narrative.
6. **Cash App Pay merchant count disclosure.** Last disclosed ~25K; growth rate is the critical unknown for the proprietary payment protocol thesis.

## Key Monitoring
1. Credit-adjusted GP growth rate (reported vs. loss-burdened) — the single most important analytical point.
2. Neighborhoods density/engagement post-subsidy — merchant retention, order volume, any GPV disclosure.
3. Borrow loss rates and origination growth — watch for acceleration into deteriorating credit quality.
4. PBA/Green conversion rates — deposit-led relationship growth vs. credit-first model persistence.
5. Stablecoin and ETF product launches — non-credit revenue diversification.
6. Cash Mobile status — de-emphasized in May APK diff; dead, restructured, or server-driven?
7. ACP/UCP merchant adoption velocity vs. Block's proprietary MRI/stablecoin rail.
