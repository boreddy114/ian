### Keystone Moat: Physical Hardware Tether

The body camera is not a hardware business — it is a physical data ingestion gateway generating verification-grade K_IP which agents cannot replicate. Remove the hardware and the entire moat chain collapses:
- No hardware → no exclusive data ingestion
- No data → no Evidence.com system of record
- No SoR → no judicial network effects
- No network → no coordination equilibrium

### K_IP Bifurcation: ~30% Execution / ~70% Verification

**Verification-grade (durable, compounding):**
- Chain-of-custody audit trails (tamper-proof evidence provenance)
- Redaction accuracy logs (PII detection failures, edge cases)
- Dispute resolution histories (evidence challenges in court)
- Judicial submission records (what evidence gets admitted/challenged/excluded)
- Officer use-of-force documentation with outcome verification
- Cross-agency evidence sharing patterns (Axon Justice → prosecutors)

**Execution-grade (vulnerable to convergence):**
- Transcription training data (audio→text models)
- Standard report formatting templates
- Routine categorization/tagging
- Basic operational routing/scheduling

### Why AXON ≠ SaaS

The market prices AXON alongside pure SaaS businesses facing abundance-driven disruption. The framework identifies AXON as a hardware-tethered verification platform with coordination-equilibrium lock-in — the **opposite** of disintermediable application software. Key distinction: application software companies (ServiceNow, Salesforce, Adobe) face legitimate agentic threat because orchestration platforms can connect to their APIs. AXON does not because the data never exists outside its hardware-to-cloud pipeline. You cannot build a wrapper agent on top of body camera footage you don't have.

## February 2026 — IC Framework: Scarcity, Straddlers & Physical Tethers

**Source:** Scarcity Straddlers Physical Tethers IC Memo.docx

### AXON's Position in the Framework

AXON is classified as a **Hybrid: Software + Physical Tether** in the IC memo's portfolio construction framework.

| Layer | AXON's Position |
|---|---|
| Software / Platform | Evidence.com, Axon Justice, Draft One AI, cloud evidence management |
| Physical Tether (Why Ownable) | Body cameras, TASER, fleet cameras, drones — physical sensor network generating data no software competitor can access |

### Framework Logic

The IC memo establishes three zones:
- **Zone 1 (Scarcity):** Physical bottleneck cascade supporting AI infrastructure
- **Zone 2 (Straddlers):** AMZN, GOOGL, MSFT — win regardless of scenario
- **Zone 3 (Abundance):** Pure software — avoid

AXON sits in the **Physical Tether** category that makes the framework investable at scale. The thesis: there is a large universe of companies tethered to irreplaceable physical assets where AI is additive (makes operations more efficient) but cannot substitute for the asset itself. AXON's body cameras are the physical tether; Draft One and Evidence.com are the software that gets better with AI but depends entirely on the hardware-generated data.

### Key IC Memo Context

The payments precedent (PayPal, Adyen, Block all to 6–8x earnings) is the warning that picking winners within a disrupted software category is a fool's game. AXON is explicitly carved out of this risk because the physical tether means AI agents cannot bootstrap a competing data pipeline. The partial exceptions in payments (Visa, Mastercard) survived because they had a genuine physical tether — 250M+ merchant terminals and regulatory licenses. AXON's tether operates through the same logic in a different domain.
