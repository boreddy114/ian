## One-Liner
Visa is a scale-economies-shared business with a four-party network moat that AI is making wider, not narrower. VAS (value-added services) hit 30% of net revenue growing 27% CC — the fastest-adopted products are AI-embedded (Visa LLM, Stand-In, VPI) — validating the thesis that consensus underwrites Visa as a 9-10% compounder when the right number is 11-13%. Largest quarterly buyback in company history ($7.9B) + guide raise signal management conviction at the bottom of the historical valuation band.

## Bull Case
1. **VAS is a second growth engine.** 30% of net revenue, 27% CC growth. AI-embedded products (Visa LLM, Smarter Stand-In, VPI, VCAS) are the fastest-adopted line. If VAS sustains in the 20s, the BMO thesis ($365 PT) is conservative.
2. **Pismo validates the M&A thesis.** Wells Fargo committed to migrate to Pismo core banking — first major US bank on the platform. 15 new countries since acquisition. Cloud-native core banking modernization is a multi-decade TAM.
3. **Cross-border diversifying.** E-commerce now a bigger share of cross-border than travel (+13% Q2, +14% April). MEIA weakness offset by US inbound recovery and FIFA-driven LatAm strength.
4. **Stablecoin positioning is substantive.** 160 programs globally, payments volume +200% YoY, $7B annualized settlement run-rate across 9 blockchains. Visa is a validator on Tempo and super-validator on Canton.
5. **Agentic commerce bridge.** Visa CLI proof-of-concept for AI agent payments. McInerney: bridge layer "has very similar economics to the products we have today." Trust is the limiting factor, not compute — and Visa owns trust.
6. **Capital allocation at the right price.** $7.9B Q2 buyback (largest ever) + $1.3B dividend. New $20B authorization (~$33B total capacity). Buying at ~19.5x 2-year forward — bottom of historical valuation band.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Cross-border decel could be structural.** +11% CC ex-intra-EU was the only line that wasn't a beat. April +9% headline requires Ramadan timing adjustment to normalize to Feb. If May-June doesn't recover to 11-12%, the bear narrative reasserts.
2. **Q3 EPS decel risk.** Guided mid-to-high single-digit EPS growth for Q3 (mechanical: incentive lapping, vol comp, FIFA opex). Market may not parse the mechanics if the print lands soft.
3. **VAS cyclicality.** Q2's 27% includes Olympic activation and deal timing. Suh flagged some CMS one-time items won't recur. If FY27 normalizes to 18-20%, upside vs. consensus is smaller.
4. **Payments nationalism.** Wero, digital euro, European sovereignty initiatives are a multi-year overhang on the most important international market. 38 countries/29 offices/6,000 employees is the defense, but it caps multiple expansion.
5. **Regulatory overhang.** Durbin-like expansion, interchange regulation, antitrust — persistent political risk for any dominant payment network.
6. **Valuation not cheap in absolute terms.** ~32x NTM P/E for a LDD revenue grower requires sustained VAS acceleration to justify.

## Valuation
- **NTM P/E:** ~32x
- **2-year forward P/E:** ~19.5x non-GAAP (near -1SD of L4Y per BMO)
- **F2Q26 EPS:** $3.31 non-GAAP (+20% YoY CC)
- **FY26 guide (raised):** Net rev LDD-to-low-teens (upper half); EPS low-teens
- **FCF characteristics:** Minimal capex, >90% FCF conversion, total capital return yield ~6-7% annualized
- **Sell-side:** BMO OP/$365; Evercore In-Line/$340 base

## Catalyst Path
1. **Q3 FY26 print (~July 2026):** Cross-border recovery to 11-12% validates Ramadan timing argument. VAS run-rate ex-FIFA. Q4 step-up confirmation.
2. **May-June cross-border data:** Real-time test of whether MEIA decel is timing or structural.
3. **Pismo Tier-1 banking wins.** Second major bank signing would de-risk the M&A thesis.
4. **FIFA World Cup activations:** Working flywheel already demonstrated (20M cards, 10% lift in actives, $10M VAS revenue in 3 months from one LatAm client).
5. **Stablecoin settlement scale.** $7B annualized → watch for $10B+ run-rate and additional blockchain integrations.
6. **Agentic commerce adoption.** Visa CLI moves from proof-of-concept to production deployment.

## Key Monitoring
1. Cross-border ex-intra-EU monthly trend — the single most important near-term data point.
2. VAS growth rate ex-one-time items — sustainable 20%+ is the thesis; below 20% changes the math.
3. Pismo customer pipeline and revenue contribution within Other revenue line.
4. Stablecoin program count and settlement volume trajectory.
5. Incentive growth rate vs. revenue growth — the client incentives line is the quality check on revenue durability.
6. European regulatory developments — Wero adoption, digital euro timeline, interchange proposals.
