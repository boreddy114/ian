## One-Liner
FCX is the largest publicly traded copper producer and "America's Copper Champion," owning large-scale, long-life, low-cost reserves that position it as a strategic asset beneficiary as copper transitions from cyclical commodity to infrastructure-critical input for electrification, AI power buildout, grid expansion, and defense re-industrialization.

## Bull Case
1. **Copper reprices as strategic infrastructure asset.** Inventory-price divergence (inventories doubled to 1M+ MT, prices at records) signals behavioral transition from cyclical commodity to forward-secured strategic input — same pattern as GPUs, European LNG, and uranium.
2. **Grasberg restart unlocks suppressed earnings.** 2027–2028 run rate of 1.6B lbs Cu and 1.3M oz Au drives EBITDA recovery to >$15.5B average. Base case: 18x on $3.85 2yr-avg EPS = $69 PT (~33% upside from ~$52).
3. **US policy architecture hardening.** Section 232 tariffs (50% on semi-finished, 15%/30% phased on refined), Project Vault ($12B stockpile), Glencore-DRC deal ($9B for critical minerals offtake) — bipartisan, durable.
4. **Extreme copper price sensitivity.** Each $0.10/lb Cu = ~$420M EBITDA, ~$335M OCF, ~$0.30 EPS. COMEX premium adds ~$145M EBITDA/OCF.
5. **US volume growth optionality.** Leach technology improvements can increase domestic production at attractive economics.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Grasberg execution risk.** Second consecutive operational surprise. Phased restart pushes 1.6B lb run rate past 2028 — impairs base case.
2. **Copper price collapse.** Sustained below $4.50/lb with inventory destocking and COMEX premium compression would indicate demand weakness, not strategic stocking.
3. **Section 232 reversal or delay.** Significant modification of tariff regime weakens domestic producer advantage.
4. **Cyclical exposure.** In a severe global downturn, copper demand destruction overwhelms strategic stocking thesis.
5. **Indonesia sovereign risk.** PTFI is a JV with Indonesian state-owned mining company; political/regulatory risk persists.
