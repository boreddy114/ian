## One-Liner
PTC is a CONTESTED, Fortress-leaning enterprise engineering software compounder whose moat is vertically heterogeneous — MedTech/A&D customers are individually Tier 4 Fortress (compliance-embedded, 18–36 month re-certification switching costs), while Industrials/Electronics are Tier 3 Contested — with the post-divestiture portfolio sharper after removing the weakest Catalini link (Kepware/ThingWorx IoT).

## Bull Case
1. **Fortress moat in regulated verticals.** MedTech/A&D tranche is individually Tier 4 — FDA 21 CFR Part 11, EASA, DO-178C, CMMC compliance creates 18–36 month re-certification switching costs that agents cannot automate. ~55% of total switching cost is durable Category B.
2. **Divestiture was moat-positive.** Kepware/ThingWorx ($160M ARR, -1% growth) was PTC's weakest Catalini link — Tier 2–3 IoT connectivity with high agentic vulnerability. Removing it sharpened the composite and concentrated capital on higher-moat PLM/CAD/ALM/SLM core.
3. **Contract lengthening (2→3 years)** signals customers voluntarily increasing switching cost. SaaS lift 1.5–2.5x shows real transition pricing power.
4. **Elite unit economics:** 86% gross margin, 48% non-GAAP op margin, 31% FCF conversion, <5% churn. Best-in-class for enterprise engineering software.
5. **Regulatory tailwind:** CSRD, AI governance, functional safety standards driving MORE traceability demand — expanding Category B durable share over time.

## Bear Case / Honest Weaknesses
1. **Wrapper-agent disintermediation on Creo (Score 3.5):** Most underappreciated near-term risk. AI assistants could own the engineer's UX, turning Creo/Windchill into commoditized back-end data stores. PTC's Windchill AI / Creo AI are defensive but unproven.
2. **Creo's non-measurable moat is thin (Tier 1.5–2).** Fails the Fork Test — SolidWorks, Fusion 360, Onshape, and AI-native entrants (Zoo.dev, Adam) demonstrate the paradigm is forkable. Creo is in the Catalini kill zone for functional-premium tools.
3. **No proprietary data moat.** Customer data lives at customers. PTC owns IP (parametric kernel, Windchill schema) + distribution, not data. The "product data foundation" narrative is the SoR moat restated in AI-era language, not a standalone data moat.
4. **NDR not disclosed** — material analytical gap. Peers that disclose NDR typically do so because it's strong; non-disclosure is a yellow flag.
5. **AI-native CAD entrants** (Zoo.dev, Adam, Autodesk Fusion AI) targeting mid-market on 5-year horizon. Functional replication risk for Creo is real, though enterprise PLM entry barriers remain very high.

## Key Monitoring
1. Gross retention ~95–96% — watch for <93% over 2 quarters (SoR erosion signal).
2. Churn below 5% — breach of 5% for 2Q = direct thesis kill.
3. ARR growth CC — below 7% for 2Q = composite moat not translating to pricing power.
4. Non-GAAP op margin — below 45% = pricing pressure.
5. Vertical-specific gross retention disclosure (highest-signal metric PTC could add — MedTech/A&D GR of 97%+ validates Fortress; <94% validates Contested).
6. Public migration-from-Windchill case study using agent tooling = kill condition.
7. AI-native CAD entrant achieves FDA 21 CFR Part 11 validation in <18 months = red flag.
